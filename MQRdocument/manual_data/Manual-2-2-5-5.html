1.3.6.6　Tanks/Agitators
a.　Tank cleanliness specifications
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Foreign material contamination / Mixing different liquid / Microbe contamination / Allergen contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To keep clean inside the tanks for contents or raw materials to prevent any effects on the quality of contents in finished products.
What/how to achieve

◆It must be designed so that inside the tank can be appropriately cleaned. Verify the cleanliness after the installation. (For the verification method, see the description about cleaning in 1.5.)

 　
　

・Install a spray ball for cleaning the upper area of the tank. The pore size, pore quantity, position, ball quantity, and flow rate of a spray ball must be appropriately designed.

 　
　

・Open the sampling valve as necessary during CIP to prevent liquid accumulation.
　

・It is desirable to perform intermittent operation with the flow rate on the discharge side greater than on the supply side to prevent liquid accumulation in the lower area of the tank. At that time, make sure that cleanliness does not decrease or pump air entrainment does not occur due to a flow rate drop after the tank outlet. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Specify the cleaning frequency as necessary and perform manual cleaning for hard to clean areas, such as the upper area of tank, level gauge, agitator, eyeglass, spray ball base, manhole gasket, and sampling valve. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Set an appropriate slope on the bottom of the tank and install a drain pipe at the bottom to ensure no liquid residues.

 　

◆The spray ball must be inspected and cleaned at the specified frequency because the hole may be closed depending on the foreign materials in a cleaning liquid or scales of inorganic substances contained in water.

◆Perform CIP for pipes that supply gases (air, N2, CO2) to the tanks because they may be contaminated with mists of a contents liquid.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform CIP or disassembly cleaning. (For cleaning, also see the description about cleaning in 1.5.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition No stains or chemical residues 　
Task 2
Procedure Check and record the residual liquid in the tank (visual inspection, level gauge, etc.) 　
Frequency or timing After CIP to before receiving contents 　
Operating limit or standard condition No residual liquids

b.　Agitators
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Mixing different liquid / Foreign material contamination / Microbe contamination / Others (violation of law)
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To mix and dissolve contents in the tank evenly by agitating for homogenization (to prevent defective flavor, insufficient preservative concentration, or concentration beyond the legal limit due to insufficient homogenization)
To prevent agitator-related foreign materials or different liquids from being mixed in finished products.
What/how to achieve

◆Install an agitator with an appropriate blade shape and appropriate size in consideration of the tank size, liquid volume, and contents viscosity and set the rotation speed and agitating time required to homogenize the contents.

 　

◆Install a saucer to prevent oil, grease, or lubricant leaking from the agitator motor from entering the tank. (See the figure.)

 　

◆It is desirable to install a splash prevention cover to prevent splashed oil, grease, or lubricant from entering through the manhole. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Oil, grease, or lubricant used in the agitator must be food additive grade as necessary.

 　

◆In general, do not use a hand mixer fixed by a clamp because foreign materials may enter the tank through the gap.

 　
　

・If a hand mixer needs to be used, ensure no oil, grease, or lubricant leaking and no foreign material contamination for each lot.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Make sure that the agitating conditions are correct. 　
Frequency or timing Each production lot 　
Operating limit or standard condition The speed and time set for each SKU must match the setting values. 　
Task 2
Procedure Make sure that no oil, grease, or lubricant is leaking. 　
Make sure that there is no abnormal noise due to contact between metals. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition No leaking or abnormal noise 　
Task 3
Procedure Perform maintenance (including checking the abrasion and damage on the shaft and blades, oil seal deterioration, and inverter setting values, and measuring the revolution on the tachometer, etc.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition No abnormalities
