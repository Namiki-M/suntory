1.4.3.1　Microbe test
a.　General requirement
Defect mode Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform the microbe test correctly For the inspection method, see the description about the microbe test method in test methods in 1.8.1.
What/how to achieve

◆Instruments, devices, and media to be used must be sterilized and stored in a clean environment. For a sterilization method example and necessary equipment, see the figure.

 　

◆To prevent contamination during sampling, perform the test in as sterile conditions as possible (Example: Heating the sample contact areas, such as a sampling valve, with a burner.)

 　

◆If microbes are found, categorize them into types, such as bacterium, yeast, and mold, based on microscopic study and record the results.

 　

◆Specify the inspection method correctly according to the target objects or microbes. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Perform membrane filtration on products that are easy to filter. For products that are difficult to filter, use the pour plate method (Example: Juice, etc.).

 　
　

・If products are placed and incubated in a hothouse, inject bacteria into them and check the validity of visual inspection because deformation cannot be visually identified depending on the products (Example: Not suitable for mineral water or products containing many sediment particles). Also, specify the incubation temperature and period.

 　
　

・Perform swab inspection on the equipment surface.

 　
　

・Perform the falling bacteria test or use an air sampler for the environment.

 　
　

・Set appropriate media and incubation conditions according to the target microbes (Example: Neutral products: PCA 35 ℃, Acidic products: PDA pH 4.5, 28 ℃). (See the figure.)

 　
　

・It is desirable to start the microbe test within 24 hours after sampling. If this is impossible, store samples in the refrigerator as necessary.

b.　Inspection environment
Defect mode Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform the microbe test in an appropriate environment.
What/how to achieve

◆The microbe laboratory must be separated from other rooms.

 　

◆Put on laboratory clothing before entering the microbe laboratory.

 　

◆It is desirable to change shoes if possible before entering the microbe laboratory.

 　

◆Do not bring in any dirt/dust or microbes into the microbe laboratory as much as possible (Example: Dusty materials, such as carton boxes and wooden materials, and stained carts).

 　

◆The work table for microbe analysis must also be separated by a clean bench.

 　

◆Keep the cleanliness inside the clean bench higher than the microbe laboratory (Example: Installing an HEPA filter or particle counter).

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the environment cleanliness of the microbe laboratory and clean bench (Example: Plate sampling, air sampling, or installing a particle counter). 　
Clean the microbe laboratory and clean bench. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 2
Procedure Replace the air-conditioning filter (Example: HEPA filter for clean bench). 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: In accordance with the frequency recommended by the manufacturer) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Appropriate inspection results must be obtained. 　
c.　Process microbe monitoring
Defect mode Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To monitor the number of microbes in contents water, process water, equipment, and environment, and take measures for abnormal results to protect the microbe spoilage of finished products.
What/how to achieve

◆Set rules for microbe monitoring in the process.

 　
　

・Specify the sampling locations. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Set the standard value range and analysis frequency for the number of microbes.

 　
　

・Monitor the number of microbes and specify actions in advance for when the number is outside the standard value range or the trend worsens (Example: Ensuring no abnormalities in production records, performing enhanced CIP/COP, performing sampling inspection again, identifying contamination areas).
