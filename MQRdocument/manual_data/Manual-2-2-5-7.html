1.3.6.8　Liquid sterilization/Heat exchange (including coolers)
a.　Sterilization process management
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To meet the sterilization conditions defined in the product specification to prevent deterioration due to insufficient or excessive sterilization
What/how to achieve

◆Set the operating range of the sterilization temperature and flow rate based on the product specification. At that time, specify a value that does not cause deterioration due to excessive sterilization in "upper limit of temperature and lower limit of flow rate" and a value that does not cause insufficient sterilization in "lower limit of temperature and upper limit of flow rate".

 　

◆The temperature must be monitored after the holding tube or right after the sterilizer. For the latter, however, specify the operating range in consideration of a temperature drop in the holding tube. (See the figure.)

◆Monitor the temperature and flow rate continuously and record them in a chart. (See the figure.)

◆If the temperature or flow rate is outside the operating range, liquid pumping to the later process, such as the filler, must be automatically stopped. Then, take appropriate measures, such as disposing of the contents or going back to the sterilization process and re-sterilizing the contents. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・For the aseptic line, re-sterilize the equipment where the contents exceeding the lower limit of the sterilization condition flew.

 　

◆Even when a liquid cannot be pumped to the later process because the filler has been stopped for a long time, circulate the contents or warm water without stopping the sterilizer.

 　
　

・Do not circulate the contents or water with the sterilizer stopped. Doing so may spread an unsterilized liquid, resulting in the growth of microbes in the pipeline. See the background.

 　
　

・Do not keep operating the sterilizer without circulating the liquid. Doing so may scorch the contents in the sterilizer, resulting in foreign materials or flavor defects.

 　

◆Set whether re-sterilization is possible or not and the number of times of re-sterilization for each SKU.

 　

◆Choose a holding tube appropriate for each SKU to secure the required sterilization time.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Calibrate the thermometer, flowmeter, and manometer. 　
Frequency or timing At least once a year and at the frequency set by the laws and regulations 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 2
Procedure Check the pipeline switch valve operation when the temperature or flow rate is outside the operating range. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition The valve must be operating correctly. 　
Task 3
Procedure Check the temperature and flow rate visually (It is desirable to check on the onsite indicators even when values are recorded digitally). 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Before start and every few hours) 　
Operating limit or standard condition The temperature and flow rate must be within the standard value range. 　
Figure
b.　Prevention of mixing different liquid, foreign material contamination, flavor contamination, or leaking
Defect mode Foreign material contamination / Organoleptic defect / Mixing different liquid / Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent mixing different liquid, foreign material contamination (gaskets, rusts), flavor contamination, or leaking.
What/how to achieve

◆Take the following measures to prevent the medium from entering the food side or the medium on the non-heating side from entering the heating side even when a pinhole is generated on the heat exchanger (including a cooler) for contents, raw materials, or contents water. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Set higher pressure on a side where the contents or sterile liquid must not be mixed than the other side. The difference in pressure must be 0.01 MPa or above. 0.03 MPa or above is more desirable. For the manometer installation location, see the figure.

 　
　

・If a pressure difference is set, the differential pressure must be secured even during divert in consideration that pressure tends to fluctuate when the liquid pumping destination is changed. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Alternatively, instead of ensuring the differential pressure, a double-wall plate -type heat exchanger may be used to allow the liquid leaking from the pinhole to be discharged to the outside.

 　

◆To reduce a contamination risk, it is desirable to use a food-grade medium.

 　

◆Choose appropriate heat exchanger material in consideration of the chloride ion concentration and sterilization temperature in the products. Products containing highly concentrated chloride ion, such as sports drink, are highly corrosive, which may generate pinholes or foreign metals on the heat exchanger. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Choose appropriate gaskets from the perspectives of flavor contamination prevention and endurance. (For details, see 1.3.6.1 "Gaskets".)

 　

◆Install a heat exchanger appropriately to prevent leaking. (See the figure.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the differential pressure between liquids that flow through the heat exchanger. 　
Frequency or timing Continuous monitoring is desirable. Otherwise, check at the start of operation and every few hours afterward. 　
Operating limit or standard condition The differential pressure must be 0.01 MPa or above. (0.03 MPa or above is desirable.) 　
Task 2
Procedure Perform maintenance, such as pinhole check and gasket replacement. (See the figure.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally
