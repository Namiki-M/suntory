1.3.6.10　Line strainers
a.　Installation and maintenance of line strainers
Defect mode Foreign material contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To install a strainer in the contents transfer pipeline to prevent foreign material contamination
What/how to achieve

◆Choose a strainer with appropriate openings according to the product specification and install it in a location as close to the product filling process as possible. (See the figure.)

 　

◆A strainer must be made of metal that has appropriate heat resistance, pressure resistance, and corrosion resistance (contents characteristics CIP conditions, etc.). If a metal strainer is not available, choose one in accordance with the requirements of the local regulations.

 　

◆After production, check and record if there are any foreign materials in the strainer. Handling measures must be specified for when any foreign materials not related to the contents are found or the number or size of contents-derived foreign materials is significantly different from normal. (Holding finished products, visually inspecting finished products, analyzing foreign materials, investigating the cause, and taking measures against the cause, etc.)
　

・It is desirable to check foreign materials caught after collecting water that was reversed on the strainer. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Clean the strainer that was removed after production and store it to prevent no damage or contamination. Also, make sure that it is installed in a sanitary manner. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・It is desirable to perform ultrasonic cleaning or alkaline soak cleaning as necessary.

 　

◆Standardize the operation to check the strainer for any damage. If the strainer is damaged, it loses the capabilities to remove foreign materials. Strainer pieces may also be mixed in products as foreign materials. (See the figure.)

 　

◆To prevent damage to the strainer, it is desirable that an allowable differential pressure should be set more than five times the actual differential pressure.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check if the strainer has caught any foreign materials and is damaged. 　
Frequency or timing After every production 　
Operating limit or standard condition No foreign materials or damage
