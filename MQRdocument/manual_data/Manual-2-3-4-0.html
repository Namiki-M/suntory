1.4.5.1　Sensory test
a.　Sensory test framework
Defect mode Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To build a sensory test framework to prevent products with organoleptic (appearance) defects from being shipped
What/how to achieve

◆Perform the sensory test for finished products before decision for lot shipping. Also, set the sensory test rules for raw materials, packaging, process water, and CIP final rinse water to prevent products with organoleptic defects from being produced. (See the figure for a test design example.)

◆Specify the handling procedure for abnormalities found in the sensory test. (Example: Increasing the number of panelists, using the triangle test, heating, analyzing the strange odor components, contacting relevant departments, etc.)

 　

◆Finished products must be inspected by two or more sensory panelists approved locally.

 　
　

・The plant must specify the certification for sensory panelists. The certification must expire within one year, and a certification exam must be held at least once a year. For examples of methods for the sensory panelist certification test, see the chapter for test methods.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform the sensory test for finished products and other products according to the rules set locally. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (See the figure for the reference frequency.) 　
Operating limit or standard condition The limit/condition must be set locally according to the product specification and raw material standards. 　
Task 2
Procedure Provide training and hold a certification exam for panelists strategically. 　
Frequency or timing Certification exam: At least once a year 　
Operating limit or standard condition As many panelists as required to maintain the level of the sensory test must be secured. 　
Figure
b.　Sensory test method
Defect mode Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To specify the sensory test method to increase the detection accuracy of organoleptic (appearance) defects
This chapter describes the sensory test method mostly for finished products, but related items must also be applied to the sensory test for other products.
What/how to achieve

◆Prepare evaluation samples assuming the drinking conditions. (Syrup dilution, powdered beverage dissolution, hot beverage selling temperature, etc.)

 　

◆Set the evaluation temperature. (This is because strange odors may be hard to detect at low temperatures.)

 　

◆Perform the sensory test for finished products by comparing them to control samples with the same SKU. (For the sensory test for other products, see the test design reference figure in "a. Sensory test framework")

 　
　

・Perform the sensory test for finished products in the first production by comparing them to control samples approved by R&D.

 　
　

・Even in the second or subsequent production, it is desirable to use products produced last time as control samples for comparison in the sensory test.

 　
　

・Store control samples so that they do not deteriorate. (It is desirable to store them refrigerated as necessary.)

 　

◆Put products in colorless, odorless, and transparent containers for evaluation.

 　
　

・In flavor evaluation, a headspace about half the glass must be secured for each sensory glass. Also, put a lid, such as an odorless dish or watch glass, on it.

 　

◆Perform visual evaluation. Evaluation items must include color, turbidity, foreign material, sediment, separation, and items set by each company.

 　

◆Perform taste and flavor evaluation. Evaluation item: Strange tastes/odors (chemical, oil, grease, mold, and flavors from other products), deterioration odors (oxidized smells, strong/weak flavors), and items set by each company

 　
　

・Perform in the order of tastes/flavors from the weakest to the strongest.

 　
　

・For products that have a weak flavor and are subject to flavor contamination (such as tea products), perform the sensory test even one day and three days after the production date because it is hard to detect strange odors immediately after production due to the effects of sterilization-related heating odors.

 　

◆Do not perform the sensory test when you are not feeling well (because you may have a different flavor sensitivity from when you are in normal conditions.) This also applies to when you are under excessive pressure.

 　

◆Do not put on perfume or use other strong-smelling items. (Pay attention to hairstyling agents or detergents because some products have strong smells.)

 　

◆Do not eat strong-smelling foods at least 30 minutes before the sensory test.

 　

◆The sensory test for finished products must be performed in the sensory test room shown in the next section.

 　
　

・It is also desirable to perform other sample inspections in the sensory test room or an equivalent environment.

 　
c.　Sensory test room environment
Defect mode Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To create a sensory test environment to increase the detection accuracy of organoleptic defects
What/how to achieve

◆There must be no odor effects from outside the sensory test room.

 　
　

・The room must be separated from others and designed to prevent odors from entering the room through the gaps in the door, floor, or ceiling.

 　
　

・An air inlet must be positioned and designed to prevent outside odors from entering the room.

 　

◆There must be no odor effects in the sensory test room.
　

・Do not bring in any strange odor sources, such as cardboard boxes and wooden boxes, into the sensory test room.

 　
　

・An air inlet and an air outlet must be installed to circulate air to remove odors.

 　
　

・It is desirable to use different sensory test rooms for products with strong smells and weak smells. If this is impossible, perform the test at different times.

 　
　

・It is desirable not to install a sink for product disposal or instrument cleaning in the sensory test room.

 　

◆Specify a lighting environment for visual evaluation to manage the brightness. (Example: Approx. 5000 kelvins, 500 lux or above on the inspection table, checking once a year)

 　

◆It is desirable to install blinds or partitions to shut out people walking outside the room and noise from outside so that workers can concentrate on the sensory test.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Inspectors approved locally must ensure that there are no odors in the sensory room and on the inspection instruments. 　
Frequency or timing Before starting the test every day 　
Operating limit or standard condition No strange odors 　
Task 2
Procedure Clean and store the instruments used for the sensory test. (It is desirable to use different sponges for cleaning sensory glasses and other instruments.) 　
It is desirable to dry cleaned sensory glasses in the dryer with an activated carbon device on the supply side to prevent dust odors. Glasses must be stored in an odorless place. 　
Frequency or timing After using 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally
