1.3.11.6　Glass bottle filling
a.　Glass heater (for hot filling)
Defect mode Foreign material contamination / Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging Glass bottle
Subjected line Hot filling
Purpose
To heat bottles with a bottle heater as necessary before filling to secure sterilization temperature appropriate for hot filling to glass bottles and to prevent damage due to thermal shock caused by a hot contents liquid
What/how to achieve

◆Install a bottle heater before the glass bottle filler for hot filling as necessary.

 　

◆The following requirements must be applied when a bottle heater is introduced.

 　

◆Set after checking heat temperature in the bottle heater so that the temperature of contents after filling meets the standards in the product specification.

 　
　

・Setting example: Hot water type (Hot water temperature: 75 ℃, Bottle temperature after heating: 50 to 55 ℃) Steam type: (Setting temperature: 80℃, Bottle temperature: 50 to 55 ℃, etc.)

 　

◆A thermometer must be installed on the bottle heater, and the heater must trigger an alarm or be stopped when a temperature error is detected. (See the figure.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the heat temperature setting value and the thermometer reading value. 　
Frequency or timing Be sure to check before production. The subsequent timing must be set locally according to the necessity of the equipment. 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally

b.　Prevention of broken bottles or broken piece contamination
Defect mode Foreign material contamination / Sealing defect
Subjected packaging Glass bottle
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent broken bottles due to interference between bottles and equipment during filling.
To prevent broken pieces of one bottle from being mixed in other bottles
What/how to achieve

◆The centering and height of the equipment, such as the filler, must be appropriate so that bottles do not interfere with hard material parts. (Hard material part example: Guide cone around a filling valve (* Described in the details field), vent tube, end of a capper PP cap chute, etc.)

 　

◆It is desirable to install an antiscattering cover or shield to prevent glass pieces of a bottle from being mixed in nearby bottles. Pay extra attention to CSD because glass pieces are easily scattered around. Example for installing location: Entrance of the screw conveyor, star wheel, filler, filler inner side, crowner, capper, cover to prevent broken bottle piece contamination. (See the figure.)

 　

◆It is desirable that it should be structured to prevent broken bottles in the filling valve when the guide cone or centering bell falls. (See the figure.)

 　

◆If a bottle is hang attaching to the valve gasket at the exit of the filler, it may contact with the equipment and be broken into pieces. It is desirable that it should be designed to prevent or detect bottle hanging, falls, misalignment, or damage using a guide or sensor. (See the figure.)

 　

◆It is desirable that it should be equipped with a function to detect broken bottles in the filler and automatically stop the equipment.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Make sure that the centering and height of the filler are appropriate. 　
Frequency or timing At change over 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Figure
Details
Guide cone: Indicates the function part of the protection of the filling valve end and the centering guide of the bottle opening to the filling valve. (It is also referred to as a bell guide or centering cone.)
c.　Measures for broken bottles (outflow prevention of glass piece contamination)
Defect mode Foreign material contamination
Subjected packaging Glass bottle
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent products that contain glass pieces from outflowing to the market when a bottle is broken in or around the filler
What/how to achieve

◆Set the handling rules for broken bottles, including the following. (See the figure for a rule example.)

 　
　

・Stop the equipment immediately when a bottle is broken. (Automatically or manually)

 　
　

・Clean the outer surface of the target equipment by brushing or jetting. Also, clean around the equipment. How bottles are broken is different depending on the categories, such as the bottle types (new bottles, returnable bottles) and the filling methods (CSD, hot), so design the cleaning operation under the assumption that small glass pieces are scattered around.

 　
　

・Check if any glass pieces are scattered on the nearby equipment. (Check location example: Filler/crowner platform, crown cork transfer entrance, etc.)

 　
　

・Record when and where a bottle was broken and how it was handled.

 　
　

・If a bottle is broken in the filler, dispose of at least one round of products inside the filler. For a burst that may scatter glass pieces around, dispose of products between the filler and the crowner as well. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・If a bottle is broken in the filler, clean the filling valve and specify a range to remove the glass pieces. (The cleaning range varies depending on the equipment and circumstances. For example, when an antiscattering cover or shield is installed and a valve where the bottle was broken can be identified, clean ±10 valves, and when no shield is installed and the target valves cannot be identified, clean all the valves.) This is because if a glass piece remains on the packings of filling valve or guide cone, it may be mixed in the next round of bottles.

 　
　

・If a bottle is broken in the filler, it is desirable to perform disassembly cleaning on the valves if possible.

 　
　

・If a bottle is broken in the crowning machine, dispose of at least one round of products inside the crowner. Dispose of products without crown corks between the filler and the crowning machine as well. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Specify the number of products for which filtration needs to be checked at the restart of filling and ensure no glass piece contamination.

 　

◆Specify the location and frequency to periodically check if there are any glass pieces around the filler during production. (Check location example: Lower part of the filler entrance, lower part of the screw conveyor, lower part of the filler bowl, etc.) See the figure for the check sheet.

 　
　

・Standardize the handling procedure for when a glass piece is found. (Example: Stopping the filler, contacting the supervisor, and moving products in the target section to another pallet, etc.)

 　

◆Even when a bottle is broken in any other process than the filler, follow the rules that specify the glass removal range and cleaning/washing procedures for each occurrence area. (See 1.3.9.2.e "Glass bottle transfer".)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Clean around the filler and the crowning machine. (Filling head, crowning head, swivel base, crowning machine platform, etc. This is because any broken bottles can be securely found.) 　
Frequency or timing Before starting production 　
Operating limit or standard condition No glass pieces 　
Task 2
Procedure When a bottle is broken, follow the predetermined rules to prevent products containing glass pieces from outflowing to the market. (Handling the products, checking and cleaning the equipment, filtration at the restart of filling, etc.) 　
Frequency or timing Every time a bottle is broken 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 3
Procedure Check if there are any glass pieces around the filler. If found, take appropriate measures according to the procedure set locally. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Setting example: Every hour) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally
