1.3.4.2　Equipment details
a.　Pretreatment
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Others (violation of law)
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform water pretreatment appropriately with iron/manganese removal filtration (manganese greensand filters) or two-layer filtration according to the quality of raw water.
What/how to achieve

◆Perform backwash periodically to maintain the filtration function and efficiency.

 　

◆Use a flocculant as necessary to improve filtration efficiency.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Measure the turbidity after filtration. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a day) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally (Example: ≦0.10) 　
Task 2
Procedure Measure iron and manganese after filtration as necessary. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a week) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally (Example: Iron < 0.1mg/L, Manganes < 0.05mg/L) 　
b.　Activated carbon filtration
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Others (violation of law)
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform water treatment with activated carbon filtration to remove residual chlorine, strange odor components, organic stains, and PFOS/PFOA.
What/how to achieve

◆Secure enough contact time with the activated carbon layer according to the quality of raw water (Example: 7.5 minutes or longer).

 　

◆Since the quality of water remaining in the equipment for a long time may deteriorate, blow the water to completely change it.

 　

◆To prevent microbe growth in the activated carbon layer, periodically sterilize the layer with hot water or steam (Do not sterilize with chemicals, such as chlorine).

 　
　

・If steam is used, it must be equivalent to or higher than food grade.

 　
　

・Inside the activated carbon treatment device must be designed to be resistant to the heat of hot water.

 　

◆After the activated carbon filtration device, install a mesh with openings that can appropriately remove fine charcoal dust that comes off from the activated carbon layer. (Example: 5 to 10 μm)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Measure the residual chlorine concentration after treatment and perform the sensory test. 　
It is desirable to continuously monitor the flow rate and residual chlorine concentration at the exit and install an alarm or interlock for abnormalities. 　
Frequency or timing At least once a day 　
Operating limit or standard condition Residual chlorine concentration standard: Set locally (Example: 0.02 mg/L or below)
Sensory test standard: No strange tastes, foreign materials, strange odors, different colors, turbidities, etc.		 　
Task 2
Procedure Replace the activated carbon layer periodically. After replacement, inspect the water quality before pumping a liquid to the process. 　
If the activated carbon layer is replaced in a two-layer structure, install a new one on the secondary side. It is okay to reuse the one on the secondary side for the primary layer. 　
Frequency or timing Once a year or when an activated carbon layer sample (taken at the depth of 30 to 50 cm from the top of the first layer) is analyzed and found to have lower performance by a third-party organization 　
Operating limit or standard condition The water quality after activated carbon layer replacement or water treatment must be stable, and the activated carbon layer must provide the water treatment function (No abnormalities found in sensory, pH, water hardness, and residual chlorine concentration). 　
Task 3
Procedure Perform sterilization (steam or hot water) and backwash. 　
Frequency or timing Sterilization: At least once a month (increase the frequency according to the quality of inflow water) and when the activated carbon is replaced, Backwash: At least once a day or per predetermined frequency that activated carbon filter device work efficiently 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally (Example: Steam sterilization: 85 ℃ or above and 10-minute retention, Backwash: 140 m3/hr and 10-minute retention) 　
Task 4
Procedure It is desirable to check the flow rate in the activated carbon layer. (The flow rate must satisfy the specified contact time.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Twice a day) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 5
Procedure Check the differential pressure of the filter after the equipment. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a day) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 6
Procedure Replace a disposable filter if it is used after the equipment. 　
For a metal filter, check the status. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally according to the filter specifications. (Example: Differential pressure of 5 psi or above or once every three months) 　
Operating limit or standard condition The filter performance must be maintained. 　
c.　RO filtration
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Others (violation of law)
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform water treatment appropriately using an RO filtration device as necessary to meet the quality required for contents water
What/how to achieve

◆Specify the operating conditions (flow rate, differential pressure, etc.) to meet the quality (residual chlorine concentration, turbidity, conductivity, etc.) required for pure water.

 　

◆To prevent microbe growth in the RO membrane, periodically sterilize the device with hot water.

 　
　

・Inside the RO treatment device must be designed to be resistant to the heat of hot water.

 　

◆Since the quality of water remaining in the equipment for a long time may deteriorate, blow the water to completely change it.

 　

◆As necessary, continuously monitor the flow rate, differential pressure, and conductivity at the exit in the RO membrane and install an alarm or interlock for abnormalities.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform the sensory and physical/chemical tests (conductivity, pH as necessary) for water after treatment. 　
Frequency or timing At least once a day 　
Operating limit or standard condition Sensory test: No strange tastes, strange odors, foreign materials, different colors, or turbidities.
Physical/chemical test: Set locally according to the product specification.		 　
Task 2
Procedure Check the flow rate and differential pressure during operation. 　
Frequency or timing At least once a day 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 3
Procedure Specify the control items, such as flow rate and pressure, and perform backwash periodically. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally according to the frequency recommended by the manufacturer, treatment time, and total treatment flow rate. 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 4
Procedure Replace the RO membrane. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally according to the frequency recommended by the manufacturer, treatment time, and total treatment flow rate. 　
Operating limit or standard condition The performance must be maintained. 　
d.　Ion exchange
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Others (violation of law)
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform water treatment appropriately using an ion exchanger or soft water device as necessary to meet the quality required for contents water
What/how to achieve

◆Specify the operating conditions (flow rate, pressure) to meet the quality (pH, residual chlorine concentration, conductivity, etc.) required for pure water.

 　

◆Periodically regenerate the ion-exchange resin and inspect treatment water after regeneration before use. See the background.

 　

◆Since the quality of water remaining in the equipment for a long time may deteriorate, blow the water to completely change it.

 　

◆As necessary, continuously monitor the conductivity and flow rate and install an alarm or interlock for abnormalities.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform the sensory and physical/chemical tests (conductivity, pH as necessary) for water after treatment. 　
Frequency or timing At least once a day, after resin regeneration, after resin replacement 　
Operating limit or standard condition Sensory test: No strange tastes, strange odors, foreign materials, different colors, or turbidities.
Physical/chemical test: Set locally according to the product specification.		 　
Task 2
Procedure Check the flow rate and pressure during operation. 　
Frequency or timing At least once a day 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 3
Procedure Perform backwash and regeneration for the ion-exchange resin. Check the control items (flow rate, time) during backwash and regeneration. 　
Perform the sensory and physical/chemical tests before pumping a process liquid after regeneration in consideration of the contamination risk of chemicals used in regeneration (See the background). See Task 1. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally according to the frequency recommended by the manufacturer. (Example: Each water flow rate of 2000 m3) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally (Example: Backwash flow rate of 60 m3/h, 20 minutes) 　
Task 4
Procedure Replace the ion-exchange resin periodically. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally according to the treatment time and flow rate. (Example: Once every three years) 　
Operating limit or standard condition The performance must be maintained.
