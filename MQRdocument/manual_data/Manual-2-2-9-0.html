1.3.10.1　Water rinser
a.　Management of rinse water
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Foreign material contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent water used in the rinser from contaminating products
What/how to achieve

◆Rinser water must comply with the management items of process water (water that may enter products). (For the requirements, see 1.3.4.1 "General requirement for water treatment".)

 　
　

・Add chlorine (up to 1 ppm) to final rinser water to control microbes. (For the requirements, see the description about microbe control in 1.3.4.1 "General requirement for water treatment".)

 　

◆It must be designed to provide sanitation to prevent microbes from growing inside the rinser. (Example: Retention with chlorine, circulating disinfectant)

 　
　

・The pipe for rinser water must have no dead legs. (For details on the pipe configuration, see 1.3.6.b "Pipes".)

 　

◆For the quality and management of rinse water in the aseptic line, see the description about aseptic filling in 1.3.11.4.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Inspect the quality of rinser water and perform microbe control. (For the requirements, see 1.3.4.1 "General requirement for water treatment".) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the specifications 　
Task 2
Procedure Perform sanitation on the rinser. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Sanitation must be performed according to the procedure. 　
b.　Removal of foreign materials
Defect mode Foreign material contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To remove foreign materials in packaging by using a rinser to prevent contamination to products
What/how to achieve

◆The rinser nozzle must be set so that rinse water contacts with the entire inner surface of packaging. (See the figure.)

 　

◆The flow rate and water pressure must be enough to remove foreign materials.

 　

◆When a rinser is installed or the shape of packaging is significantly changed, perform the test to remove foreign materials and check the residual water level.

 　
　

・The type and size of foreign materials used for the removal test must be chosen according to the packaging. (See the background.)

 　
　

・To reduce residual water as much as possible, set enough time to drain the water from packaging.

 　

◆Collect the rinser water and install a filter to check foreign materials caught at the collection path.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the operating conditions. (Example: Flow rate/pressure of rinse water, no nozzle clogging, spraying state (whether to contact with the entire inner surface of packaging), drainage state from packaging, etc.) 　
Frequency or timing At the start, at specific intervals during production 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 2
Procedure Check the foreign materials caught at the rinser water collection path. 　
Frequency or timing At the end of production 　
Operating limit or standard condition Caught foreign materials must be categorized into groups, such as process-related and packaging-related, and used to prevent contamination to products.
