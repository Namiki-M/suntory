1.3.13.2　Can seaming
a.　Seaming
Defect mode Sealing defect / Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging Can
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform seaming that meets the standards for each pair of can lid and can body.
What/how to achieve

◆Set appropriate seaming conditions for each pair of can lid and can body.

 　
　

・Verify the seaming conditions when the equipment or parts are changed or new packaging is introduced.

 　
　

・The seaming standards must be met even in low- or high-speed operation.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Measure the seaming of the double-seaming section of all the seaming heads, perform visual inspection (see the background), and record the results. 　
Frequency or timing At start (In the case of warming up, at start after warming up) Set locally during production (Example: Every two hours in the first six hours. Every six hours afterward) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the standard value range 　
Task 2
Procedure Check the transfer state of can lids and can bodies. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At start and every two hours afterward) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 3
Procedure Check, adjust, and record the setting values of parts (SCH, BPF, roll clearance, etc.). (See the figure.) 　
Frequency or timing Before the start of production, at change over 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the standard value range 　
Task 4
Procedure Perform warming up for stable seaming. 　
Frequency or timing Before starting production 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally (Example: At least 30-minute idling at a normal capping speed)

b.　Quality defect prevention during seaming
Defect mode Scratched packaging which may cause injury / Foreign material contamination / Packaging appearance defect / Mixing different liquid
Subjected packaging Can
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent quality defects, such as lubricant (grease, oil, etc.) sticking, foreign material contamination, scratches, or dents, during can seaming.
What/how to achieve

◆Install a cover on the drive unit so that no lubricants are splashed on products. See the background.

 　
　

・Use a food-grade lubricant.

 　
　

・The lubrication amount must be just enough, and excessive lubricants must be wiped off after lubrication.

 　
　

・During the operation immediately after lubrication, increase the frequency of product visual inspection to ensure no lubricant sticking. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Make sure that products are not damaged by the seaming process. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Sterilize the surface of the equipment as necessary and monitor microbes in the seaming environment and on the surface of the equipment.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Inspect and clean inside the seamer. Wipe off splashed grease after lubrication, in particular. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: After seamer lubrication, 30 minutes after restart, one hour later, two hours later, every four hours afterward) 　
Operating limit or standard condition No lubricant sticking or packaging damage 　
Task 2
Procedure Sample the products in all the capping heads and perform visual inspection. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Check after lubrication, perform along with seaming measurement) 　
Operating limit or standard condition No lubricant sticking or packaging damage 　
Task 3
Procedure Perform COP (sterilize the surface of the equipment as necessary) on and around the seaming machine. For locations where dirt/dust easily accumulates (chuck, capping roll, etc.), perform manual cleaning or disassembly cleaning as necessary. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At the end of production, after maintenance) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Figure
Background
c.　Measures for unplanned stops
Defect mode Sealing defect / Packaging appearance defect / Scratched packaging which may cause injury
Subjected packaging Can
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent defective products from outflowing to the market at an unplanned stop of the seamer.
What/how to achieve

◆Dispose of all the products in the seamer at an unplanned stop.

◆Even for products before and after the seamer, specify the possible seaming defect range and dispose of all the products in the range.

 　

◆Perform seaming measurement and visual inspection immediately after restart to ensure no problems.
