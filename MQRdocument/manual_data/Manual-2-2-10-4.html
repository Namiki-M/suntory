1.3.11.5　Aseptic filling (paper pack)
a.　General requirement for aseptic filling to paper packs
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Sealing defect / Organoleptic defect / Mixing different liquid / Others (violation of law) / Packaging appearance defect
Subjected packaging Paper pack
Subjected line Aseptic filling (paper pack)
Purpose
To appropriately perform aseptic filling to paper packs to prevent product quality defects such as microbe contamination or sealing defects This section describes the requirements for sterile products that are filled into laminated carton packaging made of paper, aluminum, or PE. It mostly explains about the tetra fabric aseptic system provided by Tetra Pak, but the specified requirements must be observed in other carton filling systems as well.
What/how to achieve

◆Sterilize the contents, packaging, water, and air, sterilize the contents pipes, tanks, and production environment, and keep the sterility. (For details, see the description about aseptic filling in 1.3.11.4.)

 　

◆To make sure that there are no product quality defects in sterility and sealing performance, specify the management items before and during production and record the results. Specify the operating range of the parameters for monitoring. See the figure for a check item example.

 　

◆Since microbes in the process may be drawn into products at the start of production, at restart after stop, and at splicing, dispose of possibly influenced products or incubate them to check spoilage before shipment. (See the figure for an example of the number of products to be disposed of or incubated.) (The production start or restart means the timing when beverages flow into the filling nozzles. It does not include a momentary or shot-time stop during production.)
　

・Make sure that products to be disposed of or incubated are isolated from other products.

 　

◆After removing products to be disposed of or incubated at the start of production, at restart after stop, and at splicing, sample the products to inspect the sealing performance. (See the figure for an example of the number of inspections.)

 　

◆Specify the frequency and perform visual inspection for the sampled products. (Including a check for leaking)

 　

◆The packaging storage period and conditions must comply with the ones specified by the packaging manufacturer.

 　

◆Make sure that the inner surface of packaging is not damaged during storage or production. (Checking the centering of the filling nozzles. etc.: See the figure.)

 　

◆Inspect and replace sealing rubber parts, such as an LS pressure roller and TS cutting jaw, based on the specified frequency. (Refer to the frequency recommended by the manufacturer.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the predetermined management items. 　
Frequency or timing At start and every few hours during production 　
Operating limit or standard condition No abnormalities. The parameters must be within the operating range. 　
Task 2
Procedure Sample the correct number of products to be disposed of or incubated at the start of production, at restart after stop, and at splicing. (Example: 20 at the start of production, 20 at restart after stop, 10 at splicing) 　
Check the incubation results. 　
Evaluate the sealing performance of the sampled products. 　
Frequency or timing After the start of production, after restart after stop, after splicing 　
Operating limit or standard condition The specified number of products must be isolated.
No abnormalities in inspection results
