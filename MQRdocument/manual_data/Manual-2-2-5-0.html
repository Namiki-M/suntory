1.3.6.1　General requirement for contents transfer/liquid treatment equipment
a.　Equipment materials/structures
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Others (violation of law) / Foreign material contamination / Microbe contamination / Allergen contamination / Mixing different liquid
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To choose appropriate materials and structure equipment to maintain the quality of contents.
To prevent equipment failures or malfunctions due to the submersion of electrical parts.
What/how to achieve

◆Equipment (tanks, transfer pipes, pipe joints, measuring instruments, etc.) that contacts with contents, raw materials, or contents water must meet the following sanitary specifications:

 　
　

・The surface must be polished so that it can be cleaned even when dirt/dust is attached. A joint must be designed like a ferrule so that dirt/dust or microbes do not easily accumulate. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Equipment must be made of materials that prevent corrosion due to liquid transfer or CIP. Corrosion resistance must be that of SUS304 or above.

 　
　

・Equipment must be easily disassembled and washed. It also must not be easily damaged even if it is repeatedly attached or detached.

 　

◆Choose a lining agent that has no safety risk or strange taste/odor risk due to elution when lining the inner side of the equipment. Also, make sure that there is no elution after the operation.

 　

◆Use an appropriate method to weld the inner side of the equipment to prevent leaking or foreign metal contamination due to a crack in the welded part. Also, make sure that there are no foreign materials by flushing after the operation.

 　

◆Do not use polyvinyl chloride for pipes that contact with the contents.

 　

◆Keep electrical parts away from locations where they may be exposed to COP or floor cleaning liquids or standardize the operation to protect them from liquids.

 　
　

・If circumstances do not allow it, use waterproof equipment.

b.　Prevention of contents leaking
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent microbe contamination from an external environment due to leaking
What/how to achieve

◆Equipment must be assembled to ensure no leaking (including parts that are often disassembled/assembled, such as swing bends).

 　

◆If any leaking is found during production, stop leaking and take appropriate measures for influenced products. See 1.4 "Measures for abnormalities."

c.　Gaskets
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Foreign material contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent the contamination of pieces of gaskets used in transfer paths for contents and raw materials or flavor contamination through gaskets
What/how to achieve

◆Choose gaskets that are made of appropriate materials in consideration of the characteristics (pH, oil content, etc.), temperature/pressure of contents, and CIP parameters. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Do not use silicone for any parts that have a flavor contamination risk.

 　

◆List the types of gaskets used in all the areas of the equipment and specify the assembly method and inspection/replacement frequency for each type. (See the figure.)

◆Indicate the maximum storage period of gaskets and do not use expired ones.

 　

◆Store gaskets at room temperature without being exposed to direct sunlight. Avoid a location in high temperature such as near a steam pipe.

 　

◆Make sure that there are no strange odors in the storage location to prevent flavor contamination to gaskets.

 　

◆Clearly indicate the name and size of each stored gasket to prevent misuse. (See the figure.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Inspect and replace gaskets according to the predetermined rules. 　
Make sure that there are no burrs or damage in the installation position to prevent gaskets from being damaged during replacement. 　
Check the deterioration state of the old gasket after replacement and change the inspection/replacement intervals as necessary. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Figure
Details
Gaskets without appropriate heat or chemical resistance may deteriorate, causing generated foreign materials to be mixed in products. Gaskets made of materials that easily absorb oil components, such as limonene, also may swell when attached and deteriorate due to a strong force from the assembled part of the equipment.
Unless appropriate gaskets are used, flavors may remain inside them and leak out in the next production, resulting in flavor contamination.
d.　Glass/hard plastic parts
Defect mode Foreign material contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent the contamination of foreign hard materials due to glass or hard plastic used in the transfer equipment for contents or raw materials.
What/how to achieve

◆Do not use glass or hard plastic in the transfer equipment for contents or raw materials as much as possible. If sight glass, tank window, or UV lamp needs to be used, take the following measures:

 　
　

・Choose material with appropriate heat resistance, chemical resistance, and pressure resistance. Also, set the replacement frequency to prevent part deterioration.

 　
　

・Control the liquid pressure to prevent damage.

 　
　

・To prevent damage, do not use glass or hard plastic for moving parts or in locations (flexible hose, etc.) to which strong stress is applied.

 　
　

・Install a filter on the downstream side to prevent foreign material contamination to finished products in the case of damage.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Make sure that the glass or hard plastic is not cracked or damaged. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 2
Procedure Periodically replace parts made of glass or hard plastic with new ones. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally
