1.3.5.1　Weighing/mixing
a.　Weighing/mixing
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Others (violation of law) / Foreign material contamination / Microbe contamination / Allergen contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To use raw materials with appropriate quality and weigh and mix them correctly to produce contents in accordance with the product specifications
What/how to achieve

◆Use a production order sheet that indicates the raw material names, quantities, and handling method and order as necessary for each production lot during weighing/mixing.

 　

◆The lot code, usage volume, and date must be recorded for each raw material and traceable (including a case where one pack of raw materials is used over multiple days).

 　

◆Check if any abnormalities are found in raw materials before use (packaging damage, strange odors, discoloration, foreign material contamination, etc.). (See the figure.)

 　

◆Check the expiration date of each raw material before use and do not use it if it has expired.

 　
　

・For frozen raw materials particularly, check the expiration date after start of defrosting.

 　

◆Containers or instruments used for weighing must be appropriate for the characteristics of raw materials and be approved by the local laws and regulations as food purposes.

 　

◆The openings of unpacked raw materials that are waiting to be mixed must be sealed to prevent quality defects, such as foreign material contamination or flavor contamination.

 　

◆To prevent incorrect insert, group raw materials for each batch using pallets or baskets so that they are not mixed in the raw materials of another batch. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Make sure that the description written on the raw materials matches the description in the production order sheet before weighing. (See the figure.)

◆Perform taring before weighing.

 　

◆Make sure that the actual weighing value matches the value in the production order sheet, even significant figures. (See the figure.)

◆Write down necessary information on the container after weighing, such as raw material names, weight, and batch number for using, or attach a QR code that indicates the information to the container.

 　

◆Make sure that the tank is empty before inserting raw materials or pumping a liquid for each batch.

 　

◆Insert the raw materials according to the method and order written in the production order sheet. At that time, make sure that the raw materials and their quantities are correct for each insert. (See the figure.)

◆Rinse the container for raw materials thoroughly after use and make sure that all the raw materials are fully used. Similarly, push the raw material solution poured through pipelines with water to ensure all solution is added into the tank. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Water used to rinse the containers for raw materials must be the same quality as contents water, and microbes must be controlled appropriately in the pipeline (Example: Periodic sterilization).

 　

◆It is desirable to make sure that the number of empty containers after insert matches the description in the production order sheet. (See the figure.)

 　

◆After inserting raw materials, agitate them under the specified conditions and make sure that they are fully dissolved. (For the details on agitators and agitating conditions, see the description about tanks and agitators in 1.3.6.6.)

 　

◆Inspect the extract and preparation (sensory test, physical/chemical test, microbe test, etc.) to make sure that they are within the specifications.

(For details, see the description about the physical/chemical test in 1. 1.4.3 and the sensory test in 1. 1.4.4.)		 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure If a new product is produced or content is changed, register the product specification information to the system and update it to the check sheet. 　
Double-check to confirm that the registered information is correct. 　
Frequency or timing Before the first production 　
Operating limit or standard condition The limit/condition must match the description in the product specification. 　
Task 2
Procedure Double-check the calculation value if the actual insert volume is manually calculated based on the basic mixture volume. 　
Frequency or timing Before weighing or insert for each production 　
Operating limit or standard condition The actual insert volume must be correct. 　
Task 3
Procedure Calibrate the weighing instruments, such as the scale. 　
Frequency or timing At least once a year 　
Operating limit or standard condition Calibration must be completed. 　
Task 4
Procedure Install the weighing scale at height or in a level position so that precise weighing is conducted. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition The scale must be installed appropriately. 　
Task 5
Procedure Clean and replace the pipes and hoses for rinsing the containers for raw materials. 　
It is desirable to perform the microbe test for rinse water. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition The number of microbes must be within the standard value range.

b.　Sanitation management and prevention of cross-contamination between raw materials
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Others (violation of law) / Foreign material contamination / Microbe contamination / Allergen contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To keep the weighing and mixing areas sanitary to prevent the quality defects of contents during operation
To prevent cross-contamination between raw materials due to weighing/mixing
What/how to achieve

◆The weighing and mixing areas must be kept clean (such as not leaving the door or window open, making sure that there are no holes on the walls, or preventing pests from entering the areas). (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Pay attention to areas where contents and raw materials are left open, such as on the manhole of the tank or the weighing area, because there is a high contamination risk. (Air outlet installation position, etc.) (See the figure.)

 　

◆Raw materials, storage containers, and instruments must be handled in a sanitary manner (such as not touching with bare hands).

 　
　

・Place raw materials on pallets or table, instead of placing them directly on the floor. (See other illustrations.)

 　

◆Different containers and instruments must be used for each raw material or each flavor type according to the risk during weighing/mixing. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Dedicated containers, instruments, and clothes (apron, gloves, etc.) must be used for handling allergen raw materials and be stored separately from those for other raw materials.

 　
　

・It is desirable that the weighing location, container/instrument cleaning location, and cleaning tools such as a sponge, should be dedicated to allergen.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Clean the weighing/mixing areas. 　
Wash the containers and instruments to be clean. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition The areas must be clean. 　
Task 2
Procedure Make sure that the containers, instruments, and clothes are kept in appropriate locations. 　
Frequency or timing After insert/weighing 　
Operating limit or standard condition The equipment must be stored in the predetermined locations. 　
Figure
c.　Prevention of foreign material contamination
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Others (violation of law) / Foreign material contamination / Microbe contamination / Allergen contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent foreign material contamination during weighing/mixing
What/how to achieve

◆Specify instruments that can be brought in and instruments that can be stored and manage them by specifying their numbers and locations to prevent accidental falls or mixture when inserting raw materials.

 　

◆Make sure that instruments to be used are not damaged before weighing/mixing to prevent broken pieces from being mixed in contents.

 　

◆Make sure that a cable tie or lid does not accidentally fall into raw materials when their container is opened.

 　

◆A manhole mesh or inline strainer must be installed as necessary to catch mixed foreign materials. (For details on the operation and maintenance management, see the description about inline strainers in 1.3.6.13.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the number of brought-in instruments and the number of stored instruments in the weighing/mixing areas. 　
Check the instruments for any damage (bags, gloves, etc.). 　
Frequency or timing Before and after weighing/mixing 　
Operating limit or standard condition The numbers must match the specified ones. No damage 　
d.　Storage management of finished syrup
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent the quality of contents from deteriorating, such as microbe spoilage or oxidation, due to the retention of a contents liquid during production
What/how to achieve

◆The retention limit time and temperature specified for each SKU must be observed. See the background and the figure for an example.

 　
　

・Measure the retention time automatically or manually.

 　
　

・Install tank cooling equipment, such as a cooling jacket, as necessary.

 　

◆It must be designed to blow contents that exceed the retention limit time.

 　

◆Set the specification of microbes in the contents before sterilization to prevent microbe deterioration and make sure that the actual number is within specification.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Record the retention time and temperature. 　
Frequency or timing Each mixing batch 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the retention limit time and temperature range defined in the specification 　
Task 2
Procedure Perform the microbe test for the preparation as necessary. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (or comply with the frequency/timing if it is set in the specification) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally for each SKU (or comply with the limit/condition if it is set in the specification)
