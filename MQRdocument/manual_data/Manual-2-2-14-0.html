1.3.15.1　General requirement
a.　Labeling
Defect mode Label error / Different packaging / Packaging appearance defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To attach correct labels appropriately so that the printed information and barcodes can be read
What/how to achieve

◆Set labeling conditions to attach labels in an appropriate position on the packaging.

 　

◆Prevent labels and barcodes that indicate information about the laws and regulations from being illegible or misread.

 　
　

・Label must be spliced without misalignment.

 　
　

・An incorrect label cut position must be detected.

 　

◆Prevent that labels from being wrinkled or torn (Pay extra attention to transfer until glue is dried).

 　

◆Products without labels must be detected by a sensor or inspection machine to prevent them from outflowing to the market.

 　

◆Remove water drops by air before labeling as necessary.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Verify that the printed information of prepared labels matches that of the correct sample before set to the equipment. (See 1.3.9.1 "Check before using packaging.) 　
Frequency or timing When setting the packaging in the equipment
Frequency: Be sure to check before production and when changing packaging SKU. It is desirable to check for each unit of packaging. (Example: Each roll of label)		 　
Operating limit or standard condition The correct packaging must be used. 　
Task 2
Procedure Perform sampling for each labeling station to check the labeling appearance and adhesion strength (Example: Bottle drop test, etc.). 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: After changing packaging, at start, and every four hours) 　
Operating limit or standard condition In accordance with the standards in each product specification 　
Task 3
Procedure if glue is used, inspect the application position and application amount. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: After changing packaging, at start, every four hours)
When changing the application conditions (Example: Scraper opening, glue temperature, etc.)		 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 4
Procedure Collect the splices of sleeves or rolls ( splices from the supplier and splices at roll replacement) and check the number of splices. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At least every roll replacement) 　
Operating limit or standard condition The number must be correct. 　
Task 5
Procedure Clean inside the labeler and the label transfer surface. (Resin pieces and attached glue, particularly) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: When changing packaging, at the start of production, and every eight hours) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Cleaning must be completed. 　
Task 6
Procedure To remove water drops, it is desirable to check the air spraying state and pay attention to remaining or reattached water drops. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: After changing packaging, at the start of production, and every eight hours) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally

b.　Measures for abnormalities
Defect mode Label error / Different packaging / Packaging appearance defect / Scratched packaging which may cause injury
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent quality defects when an abnormality is found in the labeler
What/how to achieve

◆All products without labels in the labeler must be removed at an unplanned stop.

 　
　

・It is desirable to remove all of the label pieces and fallen labels in the labeler before restarting the production.

 　

◆If the cutter is damaged, collect the broken pieces. Pay attention to the cutter blades, especially for the blade type cutter.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Periodically replace the cutter blades according to the total operating hours. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (or frequency recommended by the manufacturer) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally
