1.3.3.1　General requirement
a.　General requirement for storage environment
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Different packaging / Sealing defect / Others (violation of law) / Microbe contamination / Allergen contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent raw materials/packaging from being damaged or deteriorating during storage
What/how to achieve

◆The storage conditions must comply with the local regulations or the manufacturer's specifications.
　

・Do not expose raw materials/packaging to direct sunlight, high temperature, humidity, or rain to prevent adverse effects on the quality related to the storage environment.

 　
　

・Use equipment or devices that can be stored in a sanitary manner and easily washed and cleaned.

 　

◆Different pallets must be used according to the plant zoning rules (such as separating for indoors and outdoors and bringing in to clean areas).

 　

◆The storage location must be periodically cleaned.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure For storage areas that require air-conditioning, check the temperature and humidity. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally, at least once a day 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the predetermined operating limit range 　
Task 2
Procedure Make sure that there is no leaking or condensation. 　
Periodically check the storage equipment, devices, and pallets for any damage. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition No defects or damage 　
Task 3
Procedure Make sure that there is no damage or deterioration on the stored raw materials/packaging. 　
If they are stored piled up, the number of stacks must be no greater than the predetermined number. 　
Frequency or timing Based on the frequency set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition No damage or deterioration 　
b.　Storage environment (cross-contamination prevention)
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Different packaging / Sealing defect / Others (violation of law) / Microbe contamination / Allergen contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent cross-contamination to stored materials/packaging.
What/how to achieve

◆To prevent flavor contamination from the environment, raw materials/packaging must be stored in locations without strange odors.

 　

◆Use equipment and devices that do not easily cause insects, flavor contamination, and dirt/dust during storage.

 　
　

・IIn general, it is desirable to use plastic pallets to store raw materials/packaging.

 　
　

・If wooden pallets are used, choose ones that contain no strong odors (such as the odor of a chemical used for insect- or rat-proofing).
　

・Do not basically use any chemicals that may cause flavor contamination (chlorine-based chemicals, etc.) in the warehouse. If unavoidable circumstances arise, take all necessary preventive measures (Example: Spot ventilation of the surrounding area).

 　

◆For raw materials with weak flavor, pay extra attention to the storage conditions to prevent flavor contamination from those with strong flavor.

 　

◆Prepare a dedicated storage location for raw materials containing allergen and clearly indicate it. Raw materials must be stored separately depending on the allergen types.

◆Use dedicated containers/pallets to store raw materials containing allergen to prevent cross-contamination.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Periodically wash and clean the equipment and devices and make sure that there are no insects, flavor contamination, or dirt/dust. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition No abnormalities 　
c.　Inventory management
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Microbe contamination / Sealing defect / Others (violation of law) / Foreign material contamination / Packaging appearance defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform inventory management to prevent incorrect or expired raw materials/packaging from being used.
What/how to achieve

◆System- or manual-based inventory management must be conducted to prevent incorrect or expired raw materials/packaging from being used. (Example: Barcode-based confirmation, ledger-based management)

 　
　

・Storage raw materials/packaging with different SKUs or expiration dates separately.

◆Inventory management must be conducted so that raw materials/packaging with the oldest expiration date are used first.

 　
　

・Specify the methods to display and check the expiration dates of raw materials/packaging.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Periodically check the inventory management data against the actual inventory to make sure that raw materials/packaging have not expired or not been mistakenly used. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Based on the storage conditions set locally 　
Task 2
Procedure Dispose of expired raw materials/packaging immediately to prevent misuse. 　
Frequency or timing Every time 　
Operating limit or standard condition No expired raw materials/packaging in the warehouse 　
Task 3
Procedure Check the SKU, expiration date, and printed information as necessary before taking out raw materials/packaging to be used. 　
Frequency or timing When transferring from the storage location to the usage location 　
Operating limit or standard condition No incorrect SKUs or expired raw materials/packaging 　
d.　Management of on-hold and non-conforming raw materials/packaging
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Microbe contamination / Sealing defect / Others (violation of law) / Foreign material contamination / Packaging appearance defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent on-hold and non-conforming raw materials/packaging from being used.
What/how to achieve

◆On-hold and non-conforming raw materials/packaging must be isolated from normal raw materials/packaging to prevent coexistence.
　

・Specify a storage location to isolate on-hold and non-conforming raw materials/packaging physically from normal raw materials/packaging.

 　
　

・Clearly indicate the isolated storage location and raw materials/packaging.

 　
　

・Separate the categories for on-hold and non-conforming raw materials/packaging in the warehouse management system (or categories on the management list for the manual management) to prevent misuse.

 　
　

・Specify the approval procedures to change the status from on-hold and non-conforming raw materials/packaging to normal raw materials/packaging and to dispose of them.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Dispose of raw materials/packaging that were determined unusable immediately to prevent misuse. 　
Frequency or timing Immediately 　
Operating limit or standard condition No unusable raw materials/packaging in the warehouse 　
Task 2
Procedure Periodically check the inventory management data against the actual inventory. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Based on the storage conditions set locally

e.　Unpacked or extra raw materials/packaging
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Different packaging / Sealing defect / Others (violation of law) / Microbe contamination / Allergen contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent misuse, foreign material contamination, deterioration, or microbe contamination during the storage of unpacked or extra raw materials/packaging.
What/how to achieve

◆Unpacked or extra raw materials/packaging must be appropriately sealed and stored.

 　

◆Unpacked or extra raw materials/packaging must be used for the next production.

 　
　

・Set the expiration date after unpacking. For frozen raw materials particularly, pay attention to the requirements according to the specifications, such as whether defrosted raw materials can be refrozen and the expiration date of defrosted raw materials.

 　

◆Misuse during storage must be prevented and traced.

 　
　

・Indicate the type, quantity, and lot, and expiration date for unpacked or extra raw materials/packaging.

 　
　

・Set the storage method and location for unpacked or extra raw materials/packaging to isolate from other raw materials/packaging.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the expiration date before using refrozen/defrosted raw materials. 　
Frequency or timing Before using 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the expiration date 　
Details
Regarding containers used to store unpacked extra raw materials
1) Raw materials must be stored in clean and sanitary containers that have lids or can be sealed.
2) If tea leaves or powder raw materials are stored in craft paper bags, release air from the bags and seal them.
3) If fruit juice or vegetable juice is stored in metal containers, use synthetic resin inner bags that cause no flavor contamination approved by the laws and regulations and seal them before putting them in the containers.
4) For granular raw materials, such as dairy products, sugars, acid condiments, salts, and other foods, store them in containers that do not absorb moisture.
5) For powder raw materials, such as vitamins, amino acids, and enzymes, store them in well-closed containers that can block out light.
6) Use containers that comply with local laws and regulations.
