1.3.1.1　Sanitation management and pest control for equipment/buildings
a.　Plant zoning and sanitation management
Defect mode Foreign material contamination / Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To keep the equipment and buildings sanitary by plant zoning
What/how to achieve

◆Divide the plant into different areas, such as clean area, semi-clean area, general area, and external area, and perform sanitation management for each area. (See the figure for a zoning example.)

 　

◆Specify areas that require hand washing before entering the room and install hand washing equipment at the entrance.
　

・Installing a hands-free hand washer is desirable. (Sensor-type, foot-lever type, etc. For details, see the figure.)

 　
　

・Installing a paper towel dispenser or an air hand dryer is desirable.

 　

◆Shoes must be changed to designated ones before entry to areas that should prevent from bringing in dirt/dust from outside. If this is impossible, install equipment to remove dirt/dust from the bottom of the shoes. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Prepare separate shoeboxes for indoor shoes and outdoor shoes to prevent cross-contamination.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Wash hands and change shoes (or remove dirt/dust from shoes) according to the defined rules. 　
Frequency or timing When entering the predetermined areas 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Figure
b.　Management of the ceilings and overhead structures
Defect mode Foreign material contamination / Mixing different liquid / Packaging appearance defect / Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent an object falling from the ceilings or overhead structures from being mixed in products
What/how to achieve

◆Understand areas where dirt/dust on the overhead structures, such as lights, pipes, beams, vents, and ceilings, may contaminate pre-sealed products and specify the cleaning method and frequency.

 　
　

・Do not install an air inlet and an air outlet above pre-sealed products (including raw materials, packaging, etc.).

 　

◆Condensation must not fall on pre-sealed products.

 　
　

・As necessary, set some angle on the ceiling of a filling room where condensation occurs. (≧7 degrees is recommended.)

 　
　

・Use thermal insulation as necessary for the ceiling and walls to prevent condensation if a room needs to be air-conditioned.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Fix any leaking from the ceilings or any defect (paint peeling, etc.) on the overhead structures as soon as possible. 　
Frequency or timing When necessary 　
Operating limit or standard condition No defects (See the figure.) 　
Figure
c.　Ventilation design and positive pressure management
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Foreign material contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To design airflow to maintain a sanitary environment and to prevent flavor contamination
What/how to achieve

◆Ventilation is desirable to be set to keep (air pressure in a room with a lower flavor level) > (air pressure in a room with a higher flavor level). (To prevent flavor contamination)

 　

◆Ventilation must be set to keep (air pressure in a room with a lower flavor level) > (air pressure in a room with a higher flavor level). (To prevent flavor contamination)

 　

◆A bird screen or an insect screen must be installed on the air inlet and the air outlet.

 　

◆An air inlet is desirable to be installed in a location that is less affected by odors outside the plant.

 　

◆An air inlet is desirable to be installed in a location that is not affected by odors from the air outlet. It is desirable not to install an air inlet and an air outlet on the same wall.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Make sure that the ventilation equipment is operating correctly (no clogging etc.). 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition The equipment must be operating correctly. 　
Task 2
Procedure Do not open a door or a window that separates different areas more than necessary. 　
Frequency or timing Every time 　
Operating limit or standard condition The condition set locally must be maintained. 　
Task 3
Procedure It is desirable to measure the differential pressure between areas with different sanitation levels. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Value set locally (A differential pressure of 5 Pa or above is recommended between an area with the highest sanitation level and other areas. See the table.)

d.　Sanitation management of the floor and other equipment
Defect mode Foreign material contamination / Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To keep the floor and other equipment sanitary
What/how to achieve

◆The floor must not be intermittently wet.

 　
　

・Design the equipment and its layout to prevent the floor from being wet as much as possible.

 　
　

・It is desirable to attach a drain pan to equipment that is always draining water (conveyor lubricant, etc.) to avoid direct drainage onto the floor.

 　

◆The floor and the drain can be washed and cleaned as necessary and must be resistant to corrosion by detergent. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・It is desirable to make sure that sodium hypochlorite does not contact with phenolic resin that is contained in the floor material in a location where sodium hypochlorite is used. (Odor substance (halogenated phenols) generated by phenolic resin (sometimes contained in epoxy resin) that reacts with a halogen, such as chlorine or bromine, may cause a strange odor on products.)

 　

◆When cables, hoses, pipes, and other joints are connected through walls or ceilings, provide caulking or sealing on jointed areas. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Install a cover, shutter, or air curtain on the entrance, conveyor, and cap hopper as necessary to prevent insects or foreign materials from entering. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・The color of a cover is desirable to be orange or yellow not to attract insects. (See the background.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Clean according to the cleaning program set locally. 　
Frequency or timing In accordance with the cleaning program set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition The condition must be set locally. 　
Task 2
Procedure Clean any dirt/dust or repair any damage as soon as possible. 　
Frequency or timing When dirt/dust or damage is found 　
Operating limit or standard condition The condition must be set locally. 　
Figure
Background
(Background)
Light around 600nm (around yellow) tends not to attract insects, so yellow or orange color lighting is suitable for the cover or insect preventive lights.
Light around 350nm (around blue) attracts insects, so blue lighting is suitable for electric insect traps.
Details
Recommended items for building design.
1)When the rooms are air conditioned, the ceilings and walls should preferably have thermal insulation properties to prevent condensation.
2)For rooms where condensation occurs (e.g. filling room), the ceilings should preferably be inclined. (Recommend ≧7°)
3)No windows should directly connect to the outside.
4)If a window connects to the outside, it should preferably be coated by insect-protection film or SUS panels to block UV light.
5)Window frames and windowsills on the filling room side should be tapered (Recommend 45°)
6)Flashing should be tapered
7)The area around the flashing should be caulked.
8)If the floors will get wet, the joints between the walls and floors should be rounded to avoid the accumulation of water, dirt and dust. (Recommend a 30mm - 50mm radius)
9)Indoor drainage pits should be a water sealing type to prevent insects from entering the room.
10)For floors where drainage does not flow to the floor, the floor level should be horizontal. If drainage flows to the floor, slope the floor. (Recommended slope 1/50-1/75)
11), 12）Drainage pipes should have the proper incline to prevent water stagnation. (Recommend an incline of ≧1/100)
13)Do not plant trees or undergrowth in the space adjacent to the building, and the area should be paved with concrete or asphalt. (Recommend a paved space of ≧3m)
14)Trees should be a species that doesn't attract insects. Trees, plants, and green fields should be as far from the building as possible. (Recommend a distance of ≧10m)
e.　Trapped insects index control
Defect mode Foreign material contamination / Packaging appearance defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To control pests to prevent insect contamination to products
What/how to achieve

◆Specify the pest control program in writing.

 　
　

・Assign the pest control manager.

 　
　

・The control program must include the target pest designation, implementation plan, implementation method, and employee training.

 　
　

・Install an insect trap for each area and set the frequency to check/replace traps and the target number of insects to be trapped. (See the background.)

 　
　

・The control program must be linked with the requirements of the prerequisite program for other food safety of the plant.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform pest control activities and provide employee training according to the control program. 　
Frequency or timing In accordance with the control program set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Specified in the plant's control program 　
Task 2
Procedure Check and record the number of insects trapped in each insect trap. If it exceeds the target value, take measures according to the plant's rules. 　
Frequency or timing In accordance with the control program set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Target value set for locally each area 　
Background
（Good practice）Insects traps are placed, and the criteria for the trapped insects index, the frequency of checking traps, and the response in case of exceeding the criteria are set and monitored for each zone.
-Trapped insects index ＝ (Number of insects captured by insects trap) / (Number of days setting the traps)
