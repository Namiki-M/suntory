1.3.11.4　Aseptic filling
a.　Basic requirements for aseptic filling
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging PET
Subjected line Aseptic filling
Purpose
To achieve the commercial sterility of finished products in aseptic filling equipment (This chapter mostly describes a system with an aseptic chamber). Commercial sterility means that there are no microbes (target microbes) that can grow or spoil in product distribution or storage conditions.
What/how to achieve

◆To achieve the commercial sterility of finished products, it must be designed to cover all of the following elements: (For details on the sterilization of contents, see the description about the sterilizer and heat exchanger in 1.3.6.10. Also, see other sections in this chapter.)

 　
　

・Sterilize water (packaging rinse water, neck rinse water, etc.) and gases (packaging rinse air, N2 and CO2 for headspace replacement, blow molding air with the preform sterilization method, etc.) that can be mixed in contents, packaging, or finished products.

 　
　

・Equipment where sterilized water and gases flow, such as pipes, tanks, and an aseptic chamber, must be sterilized before production and kept sterile during production (to prevent microbe contamination from outside due to positive pressurization).

 　

◆Control the initial contamination of objects to be sterilized (ex. water, packaging and ingredient.) and perform sanitation management around the aseptic equipment. Even when the aseptic filling system can be sterilized and keep sterility appropriately, if the number of initial microbes on the objects is large or it is not sanitary around the equipment, the commercial sterility of finished products may not be achieved. (See the figure.)

 　

◆If any overhaul, maintenance, or change is made (packaging shape change, production capability improvement, equipment modification, etc.), make sure that it does not affect the sterility as necessary. (For details, see the description about aseptic system validation in 1.7.)

b.　Packaging sterilization
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Mixing different liquid / Others (violation of law) / Packaging appearance defect / Sealing defect
Subjected packaging PET
Subjected line Aseptic filling
Purpose
To sterilize packaging to achieve the commercial sterility of finished products.
To prevent defects due to packaging sterilization (chemical residues, packaging deformation or deterioration, etc.)
What/how to achieve

◆Set the operating range of each sterilization parameter. (See the figure.) The operating range must be set after validation to prevent defects due to sterilization (chemical residues, packaging deformation, unacceptable contraction, discoloration, strange odors, chemical elution, etc.). (For details, see the description about aseptic system validation in 1.7.)

 　
　

・Set the standards for the volume of chemical residues or chemicals eluted from packaging according to the laws and regulations or FDA standards of the corresponding countries. Example: The H2O2 concentration of water-filled products is 0.5 ppm or below (FDA standards) after H2O2 gas sterilization.

 　
　

・When changing the parameters, such as packaging shape, packaging weight, used chemicals, and operating speed, evaluate the effects on the sterilization level and other quality issues.

 　

◆Monitor and record each parameter and remove packaging if it is outside the operating range. (Keep monitoring if possible.)

 　
　

・Since the sterility in the chamber may not be maintained if insufficiently sterilized packaging is transferred, it is desirable that the equipment should be designed to remove it immediately after the packaging sterilization process.

 　
　

・Specify the monitoring target of each parameter in consideration of fluctuations in the process. (Example: Since peracetic acid is easily decomposed by heat, measure the concentration of the peracetic acid on return side)

 　

◆Since packaging remaining in the process at an unplanned stop may have a sterilization defect or any other quality defect, remove all of the influenced packaging.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Monitor each parameter. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Keep monitoring if possible) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the operating range 　
Task 2
Procedure Specify and perform the procedures to calibrate the measuring instruments and maintain the sterilization equipment. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Details
Example of parameters to be controlled in H2O2 gas sterilization:
Operating speed (affecting the contact time with H2O2, air, or water), packaging temperature before sterilization, H2O2 supply flow rate, H2O2 vaporizer temperature, flow rate and temperature of air mixed with H2O2, flow rate and temperature of rinse air*, flow rate and temperature of rinse water**, spraying state of rinse water
* For some equipment with the air rinse process, sterilization is completed not immediately after H2O2 gas spraying but in the subsequent air rinse process. Make sure that enough sterilization level is secured when specifying the setting values and operating limit range of the rinse air parameters.
** The higher the rinse water temperature is, the fewer H2O2 residues are. Make sure that H2O2 residues are within the standard value range when specifying the operating limit range of the temperature or changing the setting values.
Example of parameters to be controlled in peracetic acid rinse sterilization:
Operating speed (affecting the contact time with peracetic acid or water), temperature, flow rate, and peracetic acid concentration (If conductivity is used instead, it is desirable to measure the peracetic acid concentration itself through titration because it affects the concentration of acetic acid and hydrogen peroxide in equilibrium with peracetic acid in the solution) of sterilization chemical, flow rate and temperature of rinse water, spraying states of chemical and rinse water nozzles
c.　Control of the initial contamination of packaging
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Mixing different liquid / Others (violation of law) / Packaging appearance defect / Sealing defect
Subjected packaging PET
Subjected line Aseptic filling
Purpose
To appropriately control the number of microbes on packaging before sterilization to secure the sterility of finished products
What/how to achieve

◆Specify the frequency and standards and perform the microbe test by sampling packaging before sterilization.

 　
　

・It is desirable to perform the microbe test for the swab of the transfer path for packaging on receiving or before sterilization so that appropriate actions can be taken in early stages when the specified standards are not met.

 　

◆Design so that the packaging insert area and transfer equipment can be kept sanitary. (For details, see the description about packaging transfer in 1.3.9.2.)

 　

◆If packaging is touched when inserting it or handling a transfer problem, perform the operation in a sanitary manner, such as wearing gloves, to prevent microbe contamination.

 　

◆Do not use packaging that is packed directly in a cardboard box or an unsanitary container.

 　
　

・It is desirable not to bring in a cardboard box near the insert area because it generates paper dust. (Unpack a cardboard box in a location away from the insert area and bring the packaging to the area without removing it from the bag.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Sample packaging before sterilization and perform the microbe test. 　
It is desirable to perform the microbe test for packaging on receiving and the swab of the transfer path for packaging before sterilization. (To detect abnormalities in early stages) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a month) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the standard value range 　
Task 2
Procedure Clean around the packaging insert and transfer equipment. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a month) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 3
Procedure Replace the filter for the packaging transfer air. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Frequency recommended by the manufacturer) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
d.　Sterilization of water and gases (air, N2, CO2, etc.)
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Mixing different liquid / Others (violation of law)
Subjected packaging PET
Subjected line Aseptic filling
Purpose
To sterilize water (packaging rinse water, neck rinse water, etc.) and gases (packaging rinse air, N2 and CO2 for headspace replacement, blow molding air with a preform sterilization method, etc.) that can be mixed in finished products..
What/how to achieve

◆When sterilizing water with UHT, monitor the temperature and flow rate and take appropriate measures to prevent mixing different liquid or foreign material contamination in the sterilizer. (For details, see the description about the sterilizer and heat exchanger in 1.3.6.10.)

 　

◆If steam-condensed water is used, do not use boiler compounds for the steam as much as possible. If it needs to be used, it must be food-grade one.

 　

◆Use a filter (sterile filter) that has sufficient performance to sterilize gases.

 　

◆For sterile filters, it is desirable to place two of them in serial to prevent damage risks.

 　

◆It is desirable to evaluate the sterile filter performance using the integrity test or measuring particles on the secondary side.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Monitor and record the parameters (sterilization temperature, flow rate, etc.) in the water sterilizer. (For details, see the description about the sterilizer and heat exchanger in 1.3.6.10.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Keep monitoring if possible) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the operating range 　
Task 2
Procedure Monitor and record the differential pressure between before and after the sterile filter. 　
Replace the sterile filter. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally
The replacement frequency must be set locally (at the frequency recommended by the manufacturer, when the differential pressure between before and after the filter exceeds the upper limit, etc.)		 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally (The upper limit of differential pressure must be set to prevent filter clogging, and the lower limit must be set to prevent filter damage.) 　
Task 3
Procedure Perform the microbe test for water before sterilization. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a week) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the standard value range set locally 　
e.　Equipment sterilization
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Mixing different liquid
Subjected packaging PET
Subjected line Aseptic filling
Purpose
To clean and sterilize the aseptic chamber, pipes and tanks where sterilized contents, water, or gases flow before production
What/how to achieve

◆To clean and sterilize the aseptic chamber, pipes and tanks where sterilized contents, water, or gases flow before production At that time, set the operating range of each parameter about cleanliness or sterility after validation. (See the description about aseptic system validation in 1.7 for details on validation and about cleaning in 1.5 for details on CIP/COP.)

 　

◆Specify a location with the lowest sterilization level in the process as the monitoring target of each parameter. (Example: Temperature at the end of pipe immediately before steam discharge in steam sterilization. Temperature and concentration of chemical in a return pipe of the chemical liquid circulation.)

 　

◆If any abnormality is found, such as a parameter outside the operating range, perform the cleaning/sterilization processes again.

 　

◆If steam sterilization is performed, prevent a temperature drop due to steam condensed in the pipeline. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Perform CIP and SIP even on drain pipes that may be used in production.

 　

◆Use sterilized water (chemical rinse water, final rinse water) and sterilized air for the cleaning/sterilization processes. (For details, see the section about sterilization of water and gases.) Normally, the cleaning process is followed by the sterilization process. However, if unsterilized water is sprayed even during cleaning, the subsequent sterilization process may not sterilize the equipment sufficiently.

 　

◆Even for water used to dilute chemicals, use one where the number of microbes is controlled, such as process water. If too many microbes exist in water and even remain in chemicals, they may spread in the equipment.

 　

◆If operation, such as maintenance or change over, needs to be performed in the aseptic equipment, set rules to prevent too many microbes or too much dirt/dust from being brought into the equipment. (Changing clothes, changing or sterilizing shoes, wearing a mask and gloves, using degreased sanitary instruments, performing sanitation management for hand washing water, etc.)

 　

◆Set rules that require hand washing or cleaning/sterilization more than normal (extending the time of chemical liquid circulation/spraying, performing cleaning and sterilization several times, etc.) after operation is performed in the aseptic equipment.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Monitor each parameter. (See the details field for a parameter example.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Keep monitoring if possible) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the operating range 　
Figure
Details
Example of parameters to be controlled for each sterilization method
Steam sterilization, hot water sterilization: Temperature, time (after reaching the predetermined temperature)
Hydrogen peroxide gas sterilization: Supply volume of hydrogen peroxide solution, flow rate and temperature of gas including hydrogen peroxide solution. If the equipment surface is wet before a hydrogen peroxide gas is sprayed, it greatly reduces sterilizing power, so be sure to perform the dry process using air.
Chemical liquid circulation cleaning/sterilization (peracetic acid, alkaline chemical, etc.): Chemical concentration, temperature, circulation time (Measure the chemical concentration and temperature on the return side.)
f.　Keeping the sterility of equipment
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging PET
Subjected line Aseptic filling
Purpose
To keep the sterility of the aseptic chamber and pipes and tanks where sterilized contents, water, or gases flow
What/how to achieve

◆Take appropriate measures (positive pressurization, chemical liquid pool or steam barrier in the sliding parts, water sealing of drain pipes, etc.) to keep the sterility by preventing microbes from being brought into borders between the non-sterile side and the sterile side, such as the inlet and outlet of the aseptic chamber, between sliding parts and fixed parts (aseptic chamber wheel shaft and aseptic tank agitator), and drain pipes.

 　
　

・Set the operating range of parameters for maintaining the functions to keep the sterility. (Pressure value, chemical liquid concentration and volume of the chemical liquid pool, temperature of the steam barrier, etc.)

 　
　

・Gases supplied for positive pressurization must be sterilized. (For details, see the section about sterilization of water and gases.) Also, use water where the number of microbes is controlled, such as process water, to control chemicals in the chemical liquid pool.

 　

◆Specify the abnormality of sterility and handling procedure for them (repairing a defective part, re-sterilizing the equipment, isolating influenced products, expanding the inspection range, etc.) For an example of abnormality of sterility, see other illustrations.

◆Specify the frequency and procedure and perform maintenance to keep normal sterility. (Replacing gaskets, replacing capper bellows, repairing caulking, etc.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Monitor the parameters for keeping sterility. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Keep monitoring if possible) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the operating range 　
Task 2
Procedure Visually check the equipment. (Leaking from the aseptic pipeline or aseptic chamber wall, capper bellows and chamber windows, damage on gloves for inner-chamber operation) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Every two hours during production) 　
Operating limit or standard condition No leaking or damage 　
Task 3
Procedure Perform maintenance. (Replacing gaskets, replacing capper bellows, repairing caulking, etc.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally

g.　Microbe test for sterile samples
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging PET
Subjected line Aseptic filling
Purpose
To sample contents, water, or air from a sterilized or sterility-maintained process to assure sterility by the microbe test
What/how to achieve

◆Specify the target samples, frequency, and quantity for the microbe test to assure sterility.

 　
　

・Target sample example: Sterile contents, sterile packaging, sterile water, sterile gas, aseptic chamber atmosphere (collected by an air sampler), aseptic chamber inner swab, etc.

 　

◆Use sampling instruments that can sample in a sterile manner. It is recommended to sample after production to prevent any effects on the sterility of the process or any damage to the equipment.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform the microbe test for sterile samples. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Every production) 　
Operating limit or standard condition No target microbes must be detected.

h.　Sanitation management around aseptic equipment
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging PET
Subjected line Aseptic filling
Purpose
To keep an environment around equipment sanitary to prevent adverse effects on the sterility of the equipment
What/how to achieve

◆Set the sanitation management rules around the aseptic equipment (clothes, hand washing, brought-in items, door and window open/close, cleaning, etc.). Too much dust accelerates clogging in the sterile filter. If excessive microbe contamination is present, microbes may break the mechanism to maintain sterility and enter the aseptic chamber. See the background.
　

・Be sure to clean the floor around the equipment at non-production hours to prevent dust from rising up during production.

 　
　

・Do not bring in any objects that generate paper dust, such as a cardboard box, near the aseptic equipment.

 　

◆It is desirable that the structure and material of the floor and wall of a room where the sterile system is installed should be resistant to detergents or disinfectants.

 　

◆The aseptic chamber must be installed in an environment with high cleanliness, such as a filling room, as necessary. (See the figure.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Clean around the aseptic equipment. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally
