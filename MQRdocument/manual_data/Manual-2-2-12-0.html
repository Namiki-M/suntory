1.3.13.1　Capping
a.　Capping
Defect mode Sealing defect / Difficult to open / Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform capping that meets the standards for each pair of cap and container.
What/how to achieve

◆Set appropriate capping conditions for each pair of cap and container.

 　
　

・Verify the capping conditions when the equipment or parts are changed or new packaging is introduced.

 　
　

・The capping standards must be met even when the operating speed changes. Pay extra attention to a Magna-Torque capper because it is easily affected by the speed.

 　

◆Since caps remaining in the process may cause sealing defects due to a temperature change, specify the handling procedure. (Example: Disposing of caps remaining in the process if their temperature drops below 20 ℃)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Measure the sealing status of all the capper heads and record the measurement results. (The measurement product temperature must be 20 to 25 ℃.) 　
For details on the sealing status measurement method, see the description about how to evaluate the packaging sealing performance for PET and pouches in 1.9.2. (See the figure.) 　
Frequency or timing At the start of production. Frequency set locally in production (Example: Every six hours) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the standard value range 　
Task 2
Procedure Set according to the capper setting values specified for each packaging pair. Also, check the setting values. (Example: Top load, chuck revolution, static torque, product and capper head centering, anti-rotation and back guide position, etc. See the figure.) 　
Frequency or timing At the start of production, at change over 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the standard value range 　
Task 3
Procedure Check the transfer state of caps and containers. (Make sure that no caps fell off or no capping bottles are idling.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At start and every two hours afterward) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally

b.　Quality defect prevention during capping
Defect mode Packaging appearance defect / Scratched packaging which may cause injury / Foreign material contamination / Mixing different liquid
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent quality defects, such as lubricant (grease, oil, etc.) sticking, foreign material contamination, scratches, or dents, during capping.
What/how to achieve

◆Install a cover on the capper lubrication area so that no lubricants are splashed on products. See the background.

 　
　

・Use a food-grade lubricant.

 　
　

・The lubrication amount must be just enough, and excessive lubricants must be wiped off after lubrication.

 　
　

・During the operation immediately after lubrication, increase the frequency of product visual inspection to ensure no lubricant sticking.

 　

◆Make sure that products are not damaged by the capping process. Pay extra attention to damage due to the anti-rotation. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Sterilize the surface of the equipment as necessary and monitor microbes in the capping environment and on the surface of the equipment.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Sample the products in all the capper heads and perform visual inspection and cap opening inspection. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Perform along with sealing status measurement) 　
Operating limit or standard condition No lubricant sticking or packaging damage 　
Task 2
Procedure Periodically replace the anti-rotation. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Set the frequency recommended by the manufacturer or based on the number of capping) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 3
Procedure Perform COP (sterilize the surface of the equipment as necessary) on and around the capper. For locations where dirt/dust easily accumulates (chuck, cap disk, etc.), perform manual cleaning or disassembly cleaning as necessary. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At the end of production, after maintenance) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally

c.　Measures for unplanned stops
Defect mode Sealing defect / Packaging appearance defect / Scratched packaging which may cause injury
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent defective products from outflowing to the market at an unplanned stop of the capper.
What/how to achieve

◆Dispose of all the products in the capper at an unplanned stop. (See the figure.)

◆Even for products before and after the capper, specify the possible sealing defect range and dispose of all the products in the range.

 　

◆Perform seaming measurement and visual inspection immediately after restart to ensure no problems.
