1.5.1.1　Cleaning (CIP/COP)
a.　General requirements for cleaning (CIP)
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Foreign material contamination / Mixing different liquid / Allergen contamination / Others (violation of law)
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To clean inside the equipment with appropriate CIP
To prevent foreign material contamination, different liquid mixture, flavor contamination, or allergen residues during CIP
What/how to achieve

◆To prevent quality defects due to CIP, design CIP equipment while considering the following:

 　
　

・Install a strainer on the CIP return pipeline. For details on strainer design and maintenance management, see the description about line strainers in 1.3.6.

 　
　

・Install an interlock to prevent the process from going to the next step automatically when the CIP parameters are outside the control range.

 　
　

・It is desirable that tank's outlets should be arranged in the order of rinse water, hot water, and CIP chemical and a mix proof valve should be used for the exit if the CIP chemical liquid tank and CIP rinse water are connected using the same pipeline configuration. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・To prevent a remaining CIP chemical liquid or increase the rinsing efficiency of water rinsing, it is desirable to install a valve in an appropriate location of the CIP supply line branch and CIP return line junction. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・It is desirable not to make the pipes or tanks closed near the pipe where CIP is being performed. A valve malfunction may occur because the pressure rises in the closed part due to the heat transfer of the CIP liquid. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Set a time that products can be continuously produced without CIP in consideration of microbe growth or product quality deterioration (Example: Flavor defects, foreign material contamination such as scorches) for each SKU.

 　

◆Set CIP parameters based on a combination of products before and after CIP. Also, specify the procedure for enhanced CIP, which is performed after maintenance or a long-time stop.

 　
　

・Clarify CIP target pipes on P&ID and make sure that CIP has been performed on all pipes where a contents liquid flow. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Perform hot alkaline cleaning to remove organic stains and also acid cleaning to remove inorganic stains (Example: Removing phytic acid in coffee. etc.) as necessary. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・It is desirable to use hot water or steam to efficiently remove odors left from the products of previous production.

 　
　

・To prevent incorrect CIP parameters from being selected, it is desirable to create a combination matrix for products of previous and subsequent production and visualize CIP parameters to be selected for each combination.

 　

◆Validate the CIP validity from the perspectives of cleanliness, flavor contamination prevention, and different liquid/foreign material/allergen contamination prevention. (See the figure.) See the following to standardize the validation procedure:

 　
　

・Example of valuation items during CIP: Chemical liquid concentration and temperature at the end of the CIP loop, etc.

 　
　

・Example of valuation items after CIP: Sensory test, microbe test (See the figure), chemical residue test (pH and conductivity), and allergen residue test for CIP final rinse water, and ATP or TOC stain evaluation, disassembly and visual check and microbe swab for hard to clean areas, and checking objects caught by the strainer in the CIP loop, etc.


 　
　

・Perform manual cleaning and disassembly cleaning on CIP hard to clean areas as necessary. Also, see the description about liquid treatment in 1.3.6, general requirement for filler for the filling system in 1.3.11.a., and the seaming chapter for examples of hard to clean areas of each equipment.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform CIP immediately after production, make sure that the CIP parameters (temperature, flow rate, chemical liquid concentration, time) are met in each CIP step, and record the results. 　
Perform CIP with the parameters set based on a combination of products before and after CIP. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Every end of production. After maintenance and a long-time stop for enhanced CIP) 　
Operating limit or standard condition The CIP parameters must be within the predetermined operating limit range. 　
Task 2
Procedure Make sure that there are no dirt/dust, chemical residues, foreign materials, strange odors, or flavor contamination in CIP final rinse water. (Conductivity measurement, pH measurement, and sensory evaluation) 　
Frequency or timing At the end of CIP 　
Operating limit or standard condition The measurement values must be within the standard value range. No foreign materials, strange odors, or different colors 　
Task 3
Procedure Cheek the strainer in the CIP loop for any foreign materials and clean it. 　
Frequency or timing At the end of CIP 　
Operating limit or standard condition The number, type, and size of foreign materials must be at the normal level. 　
Task 4
Procedure Perform manual cleaning and disassembly cleaning on hard to clean areas and removable pipes. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Every CIP, once a week) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 5
Procedure Perform microbe analysis for CIP final rinse water or water-filled products as necessary. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a week) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the standard value range set locally (See the incident for the reference value of the number of microbes.)

b.　General requirement for COP items
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Others (violation of law)
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform COP appropriately to maintain the cleanliness of the outer surface of equipment, surrounding environment, and CIP hard to clean areas.
What/how to achieve

◆Specify the cleaning conditions and disassembly cleaning procedure for the outer surface of the equipment according to the SKU while considering the following:

 　
　

・Identify hard to clean areas and perform manual cleaning and disassembly cleaning as necessary. For details, see the description about contents transfer/liquid treatment in 1.3.6, general requirement for filler for the filling system in 1.3.11.1, and sealing in 1.3.13.

 　
　

・Use appropriate chemicals for the target stains in cleaning for the outer surface of the equipment and specify the usage conditions and appropriate spraying procedure (Example: Setting the concentration of an appropriate foaming agent and the contact time with devices, and standardizing an appropriate spraying procedure).

 　
　

・Specify the conditions for cleaning the outer surface of the equipment based on the results of cleanliness evaluation (Example: Evaluating the number of microbes by microbe swab on the device surface and the stains by ATP swab).

 　
　

・Especially for products that are not to be sterilized after filling, consider the necessity of chemical liquid immersion sterilization, hot water spraying on the device surface, and spraying of chemicals that have sterilization effects for hard to clean areas to prevent target microbe contamination. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Clean thoroughly from filler to the outfeed conveyor of seamer because a contents liquid may be scattered around. (Example: Spillage of contents due to star wheel transfer, contents washed out by neck rinsing, Spillage of contents from products without caps on reject conveyor)

 　
　

・Use a brush to clean heavily stained areas. However, it must be made of a material that does not damage the equipment.

 　
　

・Do not use the brush that is used for the floor to clean the equipment surface. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Monitor the number of microbes in the filling/seaming environment and on the equipment surface, set the standards, and specify the actions for abnormal results. (Example: Falling bacteria test and air sampling in the filling/seaming environment. Microbe swab on the equipment surface such as filling valve, packing for bottle top, capper chuck, cap disk, and seaming roll).

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Monitor microbes in the filling/seaming environment and on the surface of the equipment. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a week) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally

c.　Cleaning water and chemical quality
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Others (violation of law)
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To use cleaning water and chemicals for CIP and COP in quality that complies with the laws and regulations and the food safety.
What/how to achieve

◆Use process water defined in the required water quality in water treatment in 1.3.4.a. as cleaning water.

 　

◆Use contents water or process water that has a residual chlorine concentration of 1.0 ppm or below as CIP final rinse water.

 　

◆For the quality specification and quality management of water used for CIP and COP in the aseptic line, see the description about aseptic filling in 1.3.11.4.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Measure the chlorine concentration of water used as CIP final rinser water. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition 1.0 ppm or below 　
Task 2
Procedure If CIP chemical solution is reused, replace it periodically. (To prevent dirt/dust accumulation or flavor contamination) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally
