1.4.1.1　Sampling inspection/Monitoring design
a.　Sampling inspection/Monitoring design
Defect mode 　
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To perform process monitoring, process inspection, and product inspection based on a plan to detect any abnormalities
What/how to achieve

◆Make an inspection plan (method, frequency, number of inspections, range) to assure that the products are within the specifications.

 　
　

・Specify inspection items according to the production processes or equipment in consideration of risk occurrence frequency, effects, and inspection capabilities.

 　
　

・Specify the number of inspections and the inspection standards in consideration of the process accuracy and inspection/measurement fluctuations (equipment accuracy, inspector skills).

 　
　

・Specify the sampling frequency according to the process work unit (Example: Each lot, each batch, each filling valve).

 　
　

・If any changes were made in a production process, review and validate the inspection plan. (See the description about validation in 1.7.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform sampling inspection according to the inspection plan. (See the description about the list of product inspection items in 1.4.1.2.) 　
Frequency or timing In accordance with the plan 　
Operating limit or standard condition Inspection must be performed according to the plan. 　
Task 2
Procedure Review the inspection plan according to the inspection results and trend management results. 　
Periodically calibrate the measurement instruments and evaluate the inspectors' skills. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a year) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Details
To avoid critical incidents it is very important to clarify the major process control points and testing frequencies. Each plant may require additional testing to ensure quality products. Not all the control points are shown in this requirement (Section 1.3, 1.4), but this chapter includes the major process control points that should be monitored by each plant as a minimum. And it is important not only to just have tests, but also to review the results of tests and read trend from them. Even if the results met the specification, if an unusual trend is detected then preventive action should be taken. Trend monitoring must be valued to find possible risks earlier.
