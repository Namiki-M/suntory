1.3.11.1　General requirement for filler
a.　Filling volume
Defect mode Low or excessive filling
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To stably meet the filling volume required for each product in accordance with the legal standards.
What/how to achieve

◆The filler must be chosen according to the characteristics of each beverage. (Example: CSD, pulp juice, highly concentrated chloride ion. etc.)

 　

◆Filling valves (including vent tubes and centering cups) must be appropriately positioned and centered.

 　

◆Close the filling valves to prevent leaking when not filling.

 　

◆After filling, transfer products smoothly to prevent the foaming or spillage of the contents.

 　

◆Standardize the method to measure the filling volume for each filling valve to make sure that it is within the standard value range.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Measure the volume of the filling product for each valve. 　
Check each valve for excessive foaming or spillage. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At start and every two hours afterward) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 2
Procedure Maintain the filler and calibrate the equipment. (Example: Filling valves, gaskets, flowmeters, load cells, vent tubes) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
b.　Cleanliness maintenance
Defect mode Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To keep the filling environment and the filler clean for filling.
What/how to achieve

◆The filler must be installed in an environment with high cleanliness. (Example: Filling inside the chamber, filling inside the filler room that is zoned and has positive pressure maintained)

 　

◆To keep the filling environment and the filling machine clean, standardize CIP and COP based on the following conditions. For CIP and COP, see "1.5. Cleaning CIP and COP".

 　
　

・Set the CIP and COP conditions based on the validation results.

 　
　

・Perform CIP appropriately on the filling valves. (Example, Filler cup attached circulated cleaning, heated alkaline cleaning, chemical concentration and temperature management)

 　
　

・Perform COP appropriately on the filling environment and the peripheral equipment (devices that can be easily stained, such as nitrogen adding machine, neck rinser, and conveyor after the seaming machine).

 　
　

・Perform disassembly cleaning on the filler as necessary after identifying hard to clean areas. (Example: Packing for bottle top, inner top panel of the filler bowl, snifting line, etc. See the figure.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform CIP and COP on and around the filler. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At the end of production, at the end of maintenance) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 2
Procedure Monitor microbes by swabbing the CIP final rinse water, water-filled products, filling environment, and device surface. (Also, evaluate the stain level with ATP/TOC as necessary.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a week) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally

c.　Prevention of foreign material/flavor contamination and appearance defects
Defect mode Foreign material contamination / Organoleptic defect / Packaging appearance defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent foreign material/flavor contamination and appearance defects when filling.
What/how to achieve

◆Note the following to prevent foreign material contamination:

 　
　

・Design a filling valve so that no foreign materials fall in products. (Example: Chips from the valve sliding part, parts themselves such as vent tubes, bolts, and fasteners) If this is impossible with the design, list parts that may drop into the products when they fall off or get broken, and periodically check them. See the background.

 　
　

・Cover the filling area to prevent foreign material contamination from outside.

 　
　

・Set the frequency to check and clean the valves to prevent products from sticking to the valve surface when performing hot filling of products containing milk.

 　
　

・Install a mesh at the end of each valve as necessary.

 　
　

・Gaskets used in the filler must be chosen and managed in consideration of heat resistance, chemical resistance, pressure resistance, and flavor contamination resistance. (For details, see 1.3.6.1.c. "Gaskets".)

 　

◆Note the following to prevent appearance defects:

 　
　

・After filling, transfer the products smoothly to the next process. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Set the top load to prevent the products from deforming when filling.

 　
　

・Choose gaskets with appropriate hardness for ones that contact with products when filling.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the filling valve parts and parts that may fall off or get lost from the filler. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At the end of production, at the end of maintenance) 　
Operating limit or standard condition No missing or fallen parts 　
Task 2
Procedure Check the mesh on each filling valve for any foreign material. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At the end of production) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 3
Procedure Prevent grease contamination when lubricating the rotary joint. (Example: Make sure that grease is appropriately discharged from the drain. If not, grease may exist on the contents side.) 　
Frequency or timing When maintaining or lubricating the rotary joint 　
Operating limit or standard condition No abnormalities

d.　General requirement for nitrogen adding/gassing machine management
Defect mode Sealing defect / Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Usage of restricted ingredients/packaging / Packaging appearance defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To conduct appropriate management when adding liquid nitrogen or gassing with CO2 or nitrogen after filling.
What/how to achieve

◆Use food-grade carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and steam.

 　

◆Standardize the cleaning and maintenance methods for the supply lines of carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and steam.

 　

◆Define the management items of products (product inner pressure, headspace oxygen volume, etc.) and the management items of equipment (flow rate, pressure, adding state, etc.). Use an appropriate method to measure the product inner pressure. (See the figure.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform maintenance on the supply lines of carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and steam. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 2
Procedure Check the cleanliness of the supply lines of carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and steam. (Example: Swabbing analysis of microbes at the end of each supply line) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: Once a week) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Figure
Background
When products with nitrogen adding defects were found, the inner pressure was not measured correctly because the measuring procedure was not appropriate as shown in BAD in the figure. As a result, products without sufficient inner pressure outflowed to the market.
