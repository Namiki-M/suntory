1.3.10.3　RGB bottle washing machines
a.　General bottle washing/Measures for broken bottles
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Organoleptic defect / Foreign material contamination
Subjected packaging Glass bottle
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To wash collected bottles to make them ready for contents filling
To prevent products that contain broken pieces from outflowing to the market if a bottle is broken in the bottle washing machine
What/how to achieve

◆For details on the management items for the bottle washing machine, see the sheet of each process. For an example of a schematic depiction and management items, see the figure.

 　

◆Specify the procedure for bottles broken in the bottle washing machine. (See the figure for a standard example.)

 　
　

・To prevent glass pieces from entering other bottles, the procedure must include the steps to stop the equipment, remove glass pieces, clean the equipment, and check nearby bottles.

 　
　

・If where or when a bottle was broken cannot be identified, the procedure must include the steps to inspect the products by tracing back from when the broken glass was found and prevent products that contain the broken pieces from outflowing to the market. (There is a past recall incident in which products were inspected around the area where the broken bottle was found but not checked by trancing back.)

 　

◆To protect re-contamination after washing bottles, separate the area into one for before washing and one for after washing. Take sufficient measures to prevent contamination, especially for the single-end type. (For the reference for the bottle washing machine types, see the details field.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Post-washing inspection to remove a broken bottle from the bottle washing machine must be performed by trained inspectors or an inspection machine. 　
Prevent bottles containing broken pieces from outflowing to the market according to the predetermined procedure when a broken bottle is found. 　
Frequency or timing During production 　
Operating limit or standard condition Make sure that broken bottles or bottles containing broken pieces do not outflow to the market. 　
Task 2
Procedure Inspect areas with a high risk of broken bottles by handling bottles in the bottle washing machine. 　
Frequency or timing Periodically (Example: Once a month) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Bottles must be smoothly transferred. No wobbling or loose screws. 　
Figure
Details
There are the double-end type (inlet and outlet on different sides) and the single-end type available for bottle washing machines. For the single-end type, it is desirable to pay appropriate attention to isolation from the zoning perspective because bottles before and after washing co-exist in the same room (area). (Since pre-washed bottles contain many microbes, manage them to prevent re-contamination to washed bottles.)
b.　Inspection before inserting bottles into the bottle washing machine
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Foreign material contamination / Organoleptic defect / Mixing different liquid / Sealing defect
Subjected packaging Glass bottle
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To remove different bottles, heavily stained bottles, or broken bottles before inserting collected bottles into the bottle washing machine
What/how to achieve

◆Collected bottles must be picked out by trained inspectors or an inspection machine according to the standards. See the following for the standards:

 　
　

・Bottle neck: Sealing defects, chipped or cracked neck

 　
　

・Bottle bottom: Cracks, visible foreign material contamination (Cigarettes, paper, crown corks, straws. etc. For details, see the figure.)

 　
　

・Bottle body (as necessary) : Print defects, stained bottles, white bottles, scratched bottles (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Others: Different bottles, bottles that contain dirt/dust or foreign materials that cannot be handled by the bottle washing machine

 　

◆For inspection, see the description about inspection guarantee in 1.3.17.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Pick out collected bottles according to the standards set locally. 　
Frequency or timing Before inserting bottles into the bottle washing machine 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Figure
c.　Chemical washing process
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Foreign material contamination
Subjected packaging Glass bottle
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To appropriately wash the collected bottles by chemical to prevent foreign material/microbe contamination
What/how to achieve

◆Set the standards for the time that bottles contact with caustic soda. (Alternatively, you can use the product moving time from the entrance to the exit of the conveyor or the conveyor speed.)

 　

◆Set the standards for the concentration, temperature, and volume (liquid level) of caustic soda in each tank.

 　
　

・The approximate operating limit for adding chemicals must be set so that the concentration does not exceed the lower limit.

 　
　

・If a label with evaporated aluminum is used, pay extra attention to controlling the concentration because the concentration of caustic soda decreases when it reacts with aluminum. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Set the value carefully to make sure that the difference in temperature between tanks is not too large. (It depends on the bottle type, but when the temperature difference is 40 degrees or above, bottles tend to crack due to thermal shock.)

 　

◆Set the standards for washing pressure.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the time that bottles contact with caustic soda, concentration, temperature, and volume (liquid level). 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Setting example: Contact time = Once a month, Concentration, temperature, volume, and washing pressure of caustic soda = At start, every four hours during production, at end) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 2
Procedure Clean inside the tank and replace the cleaning liquid. (This is because foreign materials, such as label flakes, accumulate in the tank, decreasing the cleaning effect.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Setting example: Every 400,000 to 800,000 cases) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 3
Procedure If the washing jet condition can be checked, it is desirable to make sure that the jet is located in a correct position to the bottle opening and is not clogged. 　
Frequency or timing Before starting production 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Figure
Details
(Reference) Purpose of setting control items
Contact time: The conveyor passing time changes according to the stretching of the top chain.
Caustic alkali temperature: If the chemical temperature is not reached, it may cause cleaning defects. If there are large differences in temperature between tanks, bottles may also crack or break due to thermal shock.
Caustic alkali concentration: If the concentration is not reached, it may cause cleaning defects. Concentration tends to drop over time.
d.　Post-washing rinsing process
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Foreign material contamination / Organoleptic defect / Mixing different liquid
Subjected packaging Glass bottle
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To remove foreign materials or residual chemicals by rinsing
To prevent water used for rinsing from contaminating bottles
What/how to achieve

◆Set the standards for washing pressure.

 　

◆Set the rinse time. (Alternatively, you can use the product moving time from the entrance to the exit of the conveyor or the conveyor speed.)

 　

◆For the hot filling line, set the microbe standards in the rinse tank for control. (Heat-resistant molds can become target microbes.)

 　

◆It is also desirable to control microbes in the rinse tank according to the spoilage risk of SKU produced in lines other than hot filling.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the chlorine concentration of final rinser water. For other water quality management, see 1.3.4.1 "General requirement for water treatment" 　
Frequency or timing Chlorine concentration check frequency: Before starting production 　
Operating limit or standard condition Maximum chlorine concentration: 1.0 ppm 　
Task 2
Procedure Check the rinse water pressure and the rinse time (check the conveyor speed). 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Setting example: At start, every six hours during production, at end) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 3
Procedure Make sure that there are no residual chemicals in rinsed bottles. (Example: Checking using phenolphthalein liquid)
Make sure that there are no residual chemicals for all the bottles in a single row at the exit of the bottle washing machine. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Setting example: At start, every four hours during production, at end)
Check for all the bottles in a single row: At least once a month		 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 4
Procedure If the washing jet condition can be checked, it is desirable to make sure that the jet is located in a correct position to the bottle opening and is not clogged. 　
Frequency or timing Before starting production 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 5
Procedure It is desirable to perform the microbe test for bottles at the exit of the bottle washing machine. (For trend management and early detection of abnormalities) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally
Monitoring target example: Hot filling (Set it because heat-resistant microbes can be target microbes, etc.)		 　
e.　Inspection at the exit of the bottle washing machine
Defect mode Foreign material contamination / Sealing defect
Subjected packaging Glass bottle
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To remove broken bottles or bottles containing glass pieces after the bottle washing machine
What/how to achieve

◆Bottles coming out of the bottle washing machine must be inspected by trained inspectors or an inspection machine according to the set removal standards. Set the removal standards according to the following.

 　
　

・Bottle neck: Sealing defects, chipped or cracked neck

 　
　

・Bottle body/bottom: Cracks, visible foreign materials

 　

◆For inspection, see the description about inspection guarantee in 1.3.17.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure If a broken bottle or a bottle containing a glass piece is found in the inspection, stop the equipment and take appropriate measures according to the handling rules. (For details on the handling rules, see a. "General bottle washing/Measures for broken bottles".) 　
Frequency or timing Every time a broken bottle or a glass piece is found 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally
