1.3.6.9　UV sterilizer for contents
a.　UV sterilizer for contents
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Foreign material contamination / Organoleptic defect
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To appropriately manage the UV sterilizer
What/how to achieve

◆Choose an appropriate UV sterilizer according to the characteristics of the contents and the microbe risks.

 　
　

・If a liquid where bromide ion and chlorine may exist is sterilized, perform inspection as necessary because bromic acid may be generated.

 　

◆Specify management conditions to secure the UV irradiation amount.

 　
　

・The temperature and flow rate of the contents must be specified for control.

 　
　

・It is desirable that the temperature and flow rate of the contents and the lighting state of the UV lamp should be monitored always and interlocked.

 　
　

・Set the replacement intervals to prevent insufficient sterilization due to a drop in illumination of the UV sterilization lamp.

 　

◆Appropriate measures must be taken to prevent hard foreign material contamination or exposure due to damage in the UV sterilization lamp.

 　
　

・Control the pressure in consideration of the pressure resistance of the UV sterilizer (Pay attention to pressurization due to a closed-circuit if a power failure occurs).

 　
　

・Install strainers on the upstream and downstream sides of the UV sterilizer.

 　

◆The handling procedure must be specified to prevent the lamp contents from being mixed in products when the UV sterilization lamp is damaged. Pay extra attention, especially when a mercury lamp is used.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Make sure that the UV sterilization lamp is ON when in use. 　
Check the UV irradiation conditions (flow rate, pressure, temperature, etc.) when in use. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (according to the frequency recommended by the manufacturer) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Task 2
Procedure Check the total operating hours and periodically replace the UV sterilization lamp (including the sleeve). Measure the UV illumination if possible. 　
Periodically clean the installed pipes, equipment, and UV sterilization lamp (including the sleeve) surface to prevent insufficient UV irradiation. 　
Perform periodic inspection to ensure no damage or impurities on the surface. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (according to the frequency recommended by the manufacturer) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally (according to the frequency recommended by the manufacturer) 　
Details
The following must be considered when selecting a UV sterilizer:
- It is desirable to set the specifications according to the irradiation amount of ultraviolet rays required to inactivate the target microbes.
- It is desirable to set the specifications according to the ultraviolet ray transmittance (liquid chromaticity, turbidity, ultraviolet ray absorbing material presence), temperature, and flow rate of contents.
