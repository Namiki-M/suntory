1.3.20.1　Storage environment/Inventory management
a.　Storage environment
Defect mode Packaging appearance defect / Organoleptic defect / Scratched packaging which may cause injury
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To store products with the quality maintained.
What/how to achieve

◆Do not expose products to direct sunlight, high temperature and humidity, wind and rain, and strong odor (Example: Store in a location with a roof that does not leak). See the background.

 　

◆Keep the storage location clean (Example: There are no water puddles on the floor caused by condensation from products, and no garbage, spider webs, and bird's nests). See the background.

 　

◆Set the rules about how to transport and store products and carry them inside and outside without any damage (limiting the number of stacked pallets, transporting with a forklift, how to load and unload from a rack and truck, checking the damage and stain on pallets, etc.)

 　

◆Set the rules about storage temperature management if products need to be stored at low temperatures.

 　

◆Note the following to prevent some odor from sticking to products:

 　
　

・Do not basically use any chemicals (chlorine-based chemicals, etc.) in the warehouse that cause some odors to stick to products. If unavoidable circumstances arise, take all necessary preventive measures (Example: Spot ventilation of the surrounding area).

 　
　

・Choose wooden pallets that contain no strong odors (Example: Odor of a chemical used for insect- or rat-proofing).

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Clean inside the product warehouse. Also, make sure that it is kept clean. 　
Check the temperature inside the warehouse as necessary. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Background
b.　Prevention of miss shipping
Defect mode Shipping non-conforming products or test sample
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent miss shipping of on-hold/non-conforming products when they are found..
What/how to achieve

◆If on-hold or non-conforming products are found, set the work rules while considering the following:

 　
　

・Make sure that the number of on-hold or non-conforming products matches the number of unreleasable products in the target range.

 　
　

・Dispose of non-conforming products immediately and record the number of them.

 　
　

・Designate and indicate the on-hold and non-conforming product storage areas. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Make non-conforming products visually identifiable. (See the figure.)

 　

◆If the factory has installed the warehouse management system, perform the following:

 　
　

・Separate on-hold and non-conforming products from conforming products and provide them with the status indicating that they are unreleasable on the system.

 　
　

・Do not provide on-hold and non-conforming products with the status indicating that they are releasable even temporarily on the system. (See the figure.)

◆If the factory has not installed the warehouse management system, perform the following:

 　
　

・Make on-hold and non-conforming products visually identifiable for each storage unit (pallet or case).
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure If the system has been installed, check the system to make sure that the number of products with the unreleasable status matches the number of on-hold and non-conforming products in the target range. 　
Frequency or timing Immediately after providing the relevant products with the status indicating that they must not be shipped 　
Operating limit or standard condition The numbers must add up perfectly. 　
Task 2
Procedure If the system has not been installed, make sure that the number of unreleasable products in the warehouse matches the number of on-hold and non-conforming products in the target range. 　
Frequency or timing Immediately after stopping the shipment of the relevant products 　
Operating limit or standard condition The numbers must add up perfectly.
