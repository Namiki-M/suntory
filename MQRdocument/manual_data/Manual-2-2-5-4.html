1.3.6.5　Valves
a.　General requirement for valves
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Foreign material contamination / Microbe contamination / Allergen contamination / Mixing different liquid
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To choose and manage valves appropriately to prevent an adverse effect on the quality of contents (For details on prevention of cleaning defect and mixing different liquid, also see other sections in this chapter.)
What/how to achieve

◆If an automatic valve is installed, choose and install one with the spring return function in consideration of fail-safe operation to prevent liquids from entering unexpected areas at a power failure.

 　

◆To detect automatic valve malfunctions, the interlock function must be provided, such as a proximity switch. Also, make sure that it works correctly after the installation.

 　

◆Specify the maintenance method and frequency. (Gasket inspection and replacement, proximity switch adjustment and operation check, etc.)

◆Valve failures or malfunctions due to the submersion of electrical parts, such as solenoid valves, must be prevented. (For details, see 1.3.6.1.) (For an incident that occurred due to the submersion of a solenoid valve, see the figure.)

b.　Cleaning defect prevention
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Allergen contamination / Microbe contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent any quality defects of contents due to a liquid accumulating in a valve or a cleaning defect
What/how to achieve

◆Since a check valve creates a hard to clean area, do not use it for a contents pipeline that requires CIP but use a one-way relief valve to prevent reverse flow. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Since a ball valve creates a hard to clean area, do not use it for a contents pipeline that requires CIP.

 　

◆Valves used in sterile areas must be sterile ones that stop microbes from the sliding parts (diaphragm valve, valve with bellows, valve with a steam block, etc.). (See the figure.)

 　

◆Install valves in a direction that does not cause liquid accumulation (to prevent liquid accumulation in the body or CIP liquid accumulation to the valve cleaning pipeline in mix proof, etc.). (See the figure.)

 　

◆Identify areas where stains are likely to remain and specify an appropriate cleaning method. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Open the valves on the blow side during CIP as necessary and perform cleaning.

 　
　

・Open the sampling valves installed on the tank and pipes during CIP as necessary and perform cleaning.

 　
　

・If the cross valve is half-opened during CIP, check the CIP validity while paying attention to a drop in the flow rate.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the cleanliness after CIP. (For details, see the description about cleaning in 1.5.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition No remaining stains

c.　Prevention of mixing different liquid
Defect mode Organoleptic defect / Mixing different liquid / Microbe contamination / Allergen contamination
Subjected packaging All packaging
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent different liquid mixture in valve installation locations.
What/how to achieve

◆Use a mix proof valve or a drain-out system in areas where contents may contact with different liquids, such as CIP chemicals, so that a liquid is discharged outside even if it leaks from a closed valve. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・If a drain-out system is used, it is desirable that it should be automatically controlled to prevent human errors.

 　

◆Even when a mix proof valve is used, the following preventive measures must be taken because mixing different liquid may occur if a temporary pressure rise due to water hammer pushes up the valve disk. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・Prevent water hammer itself by making no sudden changes in the flow speed. (Changing the pump output or operating valves slowly, etc.)

 　
　

・Prevent the valve disk from being pushed up. (Adjust the spring pressure of the actuator or install a balancer in the lower area so that the disk does not move. Install a relief valve in a location where a pressure rise occurs to release it.)

 　
　

・Install a limit switch to detect valve disk push-up.

 　
　

・When contents are flowing on the upper side of the mix proof valve, it is desirable NOT to perform CIP on the lower side at the same time. (See the figure.)

 　
　

・To isolate a gas from a liquid, a gas is desirable to be on the upper side and a liquid is desirable to be on the lower side to prevent mixture due to a difference in specific gravity.
