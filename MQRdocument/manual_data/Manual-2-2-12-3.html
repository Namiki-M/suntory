1.3.13.4　Plastic cups
a.　Heat-sealing
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Sealing defect / Organoleptic defect / Packaging appearance defect / Difficult to open / Foreign material contamination
Subjected packaging Cup
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To appropriately seal or heat-seal cup packaging (* This chapter describes conduction-type heat sealing. There is no description about peel-off.)
What/how to achieve

◆Set appropriate heat-sealing conditions for each packaging pair (heating/cooling temperature, pressure, contact time, etc.).

 　
　

・If the contact time changes due to the cycle time, verify the adhesion conditions.

 　
　

・All sealer heads and cup holders must be level and centered. (See the figure.)

 　

◆Appropriately wash and clean to prevent dirt/dust attached to the heater or cup holders from affecting the adhesion conditions.

 　

◆It is desirable to coat the heater surface to prevent lid materials from sticking to the heater. (Example: Tfe-Lok coating) (See the figure.)

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform the sealing test for all the sealing heads and record the results. (After sealing and after heating/cooling) For the inspection method, see the description about how to evaluate the packaging sealing performance for plastic cups in 1.8.2.5. 　
Sample the products in all the sealing heads and perform visual inspection. 　
Frequency or timing At the start of production. And at the frequency set locally in production (Example: Every two hours) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the predetermined standard value range 　
Task 2
Procedure Set according to the predetermined heat-sealing conditions. Also, check the setting values. (Heating/cooling temperature, pressure, contact time, etc.) 　
Frequency or timing Before the start of production, at change over 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the standard value range 　
Task 3
Procedure Check the transfer state of containers (no excessive liquid spillage). 　
Perform inspection and cleaning to make sure that there are no foreign materials or scorches on the surface of the heater. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At start and every two hours afterward) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally

b.　Packaging supply/transfer
Defect mode Sealing defect / Packaging appearance defect / Foreign material contamination / Difficult to open
Subjected packaging Cup
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent foreign material contamination or appearance defects during cup transfer.
What/how to achieve

◆Foreign materials must not be mixed in when cups or lids are inserted.

 　

◆Install a cover above cup transfer to prevent foreign material contamination. Remove foreign materials attached to the cup holders with water.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform visual inspection on cups to check any flange cracks before use. (See the background.) 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition No defects 　
Task 2
Procedure Collect the seams of lid rolls (seams from the supplier and seams at roll replacement) and check the number of seams. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At least every roll replacement) 　
Operating limit or standard condition The number must be correct. 　
Task 3
Procedure Check the transfer state of containers. 　
Check the packaging and products for any appearance damage. Pay extra attention to pinhole leaking. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: When changing packaging SKU, at start, every two hours) 　
Operating limit or standard condition No abnormalities 　
Details
Methods to prevent blocking of packaging
Cup packagings are stacked and packed for storage. If impact or load is applied during storage, stacked cups cannot get come off, which leads to blocking. Reduce the occurrence of blocking by specifying the number of stacked cups (the number of cups per stack) or the quantity per packaging which is manufactured by the supplier and then transfered.
Lid packagings are wound up in roll state at the time of delivery from the supplier. It causes the contact between the sealing layer that adheres to laminate films during storage, and the printing layer on the outside of the product. To prevent blocking (the state of being difficult to peel them off), pack and store them so that the wound up roll section should not be affected by the impact or applied load. Stand the core material of the roll during storage, or fix the core material of the roll in cardboard box, so that the wounded part of the roll should not make a contact. Also, control the storage temperature and humidity according to the packaging specification.
