1.3.13.5　Pouch
a.　Heat-sealing
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Sealing defect / Organoleptic defect / Packaging appearance defect / Difficult to open
Subjected packaging Pouch
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To heat-seal pouch packaging to prevent sealing or appearance defects (* This chapter describes conduction-type heat sealing. See the capping section for spout seaming.)
What/how to achieve

◆Set appropriate heat-sealing conditions for each packaging pair (heating/cooling temperature, pressure, contact time, etc.).

 　
　

・Packaging to be heated must be warmed before production.

 　
　

・All sealing bars must be centered and level. See the details.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Perform the sealing test for all the sealing heads and record the results. (After sealing and after heating/cooling) 　
Sample the products in all the sealing heads and perform visual inspection. 　
For the inspection method, see the description about how to evaluate the packaging sealing performance for pouches in 1.8.2.5. 　
Frequency or timing At the start of production. And at the frequency set locally in production (Example: Every two hours) 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the predetermined standard value range 　
Task 2
Procedure Set according to the predetermined heat-sealing conditions. Also, check the setting values. (Heating/cooling temperature, pressure, contact time, etc.) 　
Frequency or timing Before the start of production, at change over 　
Operating limit or standard condition Within the standard value range 　
Task 3
Procedure Perform inspection and cleaning to make sure that there are no scratches or dust on the surface of the sealing bar. 　
Periodically clean to prevent clogging in the refrigerant system (air, water, etc.) of the cooling part. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally 　
Operating limit or standard condition Set locally 　
Figure
Background
Example of a quality defect caused by overheating or overpressure:
Tuck ･･･ Overheating or unstable temperature affect the packaging and cause this defect. Pouch packaging shrinks and becomes wrinkled. Also, leaks often occur in the "tuck".
Polly ball ･･･ Defect caused by overpressure. The sealing layer resin of the pouch packaging is lumped in the edge of the seal. Pinholes or other leaks often occur due to this defect.
Torn edge ･･･ Excessive pressure or heating time causes hard sealed resin. Due to this, the edge sealing strength is weak. As a result, torn edges in the sealing part often occur.


Examples of quality defects caused by excessive heating or excessive adhesion:
Tuck ･･･ Overheating or unstable temperature affects the packaging, except for the adhesion layer, leading to shrinkage or wrinkle. The folded area becomes susceptible to damage.
Polly ball ･･･ Due to excessive adhesion, the resin of the adhesion layer is lumped and protruding. It causes pinholes or leaks due to drop impact.
Torn edge ･･･ Due to excessive or long adhesion, the resin of the adhesion layer adheres more than necessary. The edge becomes weak, and leaks occur more often when drop impact, etc. are applied.
b.　Packaging supply/transfer
Defect mode Microbe contamination / Sealing defect / Organoleptic defect / Packaging appearance defect / Others (violation of law) / Foreign material contamination
Subjected packaging Pouch
Subjected line All lines
Purpose
To prevent sealing defects or foreign material contamination due to the transfer or cutting of pouch bags.
What/how to achieve

◆Transfer pouch bags while ensuring no scraping or leaking.

 　
How to maintain/control
Task 1
Procedure Check the packaging and products for any appearance damage. Pay extra attention to pinhole leaking. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: When changing packaging SKU, at start, every two hours) 　
Operating limit or standard condition No abnormalities 　
Task 2
Procedure Collect the seams of pouch bag rolls (both seams from the supplier and seams at roll replacement) and check the number of seams. 　
Frequency or timing Set locally (Example: At least every roll replacement) 　
Operating limit or standard condition The number must be correct. 　
Figure
Background
Pouch packaging is a soft, flexible package as opposed to PET bottles and cans. Measures to prevent quality defects due to abnormal package transferring must be implemented in advance.

Pouch container is more flexible than PET bottle or can. Take appropriate measures to prevent the quality defects at the time of transferring pouch container or product.
